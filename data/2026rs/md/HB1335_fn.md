Department of Information Technology - Recruitment, Retention, and 
Compensation of Information Technology and Cybersecurity Professionals - 
Independent Study 
 
 
This bill requires the Department of Information Technology (DoIT) to procure the 
services of a third-party entity to conduct an independent study regarding compensation, 
salaries, recruitment, and retention for State information technology (IT) and cybersecurity 
professional staff. DoIT must submit the study to the Governor and General Assembly by 
November 1, 2027. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026, and terminates June 30, 2028. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  General fund expenditures increase by an estimated $375,000 in each of 
FY 2027 and 2028 for the mandated study. Revenues are not affected.

Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  The independent study procured by DoIT must include: 
 
 
a comparison of compensation of IT and cybersecurity professionals at State agencies 
with other comparable positions at the federal and local levels and in the 
private sector;

State government infrastructure. “IT” means all electronic information processing, 
including maintenance, telecommunications, hardware, software, and associated services. 
 
State Expenditures:  General fund expenditures increase by approximately $375,000 in 
each of fiscal 2027 and 2028 (totaling $750,000 over the two-year period) for DoIT to 
contract with a third-party entity to conduct the required study. The estimate is based on a 
similar study undertaken by Department of Budget and Management concerning 
compensation rates and retention for State employees which, according to DoIT, was 
estimated to cost between $750,000 and $1.2 million. For purposes of this analysis, it is 
assumed that costs incurred by DoIT are split equally between the two fiscal years that 
encompass the study period; however, the actual timing of expenditures may vary.
