Ways and Means 
 
 
State Board of Education - Public High School Graduation Requirements 
 
 
This bill requires the State Board of Education (SBE) to (1) review and update graduation 
requirements every five years and (2) implement changes to graduation requirements for 
the cohort entering high school in the school year following SBE approval. The bill takes 
effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The bill establishes a statutory review timeline for graduation 
requirements; any associated workload is assumed to be handled with existing resources. 
Revenues are not affected. 
  
Local Effect:  Likely minimal fiscal and operational impact on local school systems, unless 
the bill results in more frequent or significant changes to graduation requirements adopted 
by SBE. Revenues are not affected. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:

State/Local Expenditures:  The bill generally formalizes a five-year review cycle for 
graduation requirements and clarifies that any approved changes apply to the cohort 
entering high school in the school year following SBE approval. The SBE already has 
authority to review and update graduation requirements under current law. 
 
The Maryland State Department of Education advises that SBE periodically reviews and 
updates graduation requirements, most recently in 2022 to align college and career 
readiness standards with the Blueprint for Maryland’s Future. Although the bill establishes 
a statutory timeline for review, any additional coordination or standards development 
activities are assumed to be handled with existing resources. Accordingly, 
State expenditures are not affected. 
 
Local school systems must comply with graduation requirements established by SBE under 
current law. The bill does not mandate specific new graduation requirements and 
potentially affects only the timing of future changes rather than their content. To the extent 
that future updates adopted by SBE result in additional course requirements or 
modifications to course offerings, local school system expenditures could increase. 
However, any such impact depends on future policy decisions by SBE and cannot be 
reliably estimated; any impact is likely minimal. 
 
 
Additional Information
