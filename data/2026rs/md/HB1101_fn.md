Worcester County - Alcoholic Beverages - Temporary To-Go Event Permit and 
Class C Per Diem Municipal To-Go Beer, Wine, and Liquor License 
 
 
This bill establishes, in Worcester County, a Class C per diem municipal to-go beer, wine, 
and liquor (BWL) license and a related temporary to-go event permit. The bill authorizes 
the Worcester County Board of License Commissioners to issue the license to  
Pocomoke City, the Town of Berlin, and the Town of Snow Hill, and authorizes the license 
holder to allow beer, wine, and liquor sold by the holder of a temporary to-go event permit 
to enter a designated special event area described in the license for a period not exceeding  
seven consecutive days, as specified. The bill also authorizes the board to issue a  
to-go event permit to the holder of Class B and Class D alcoholic beverages licenses located 
within or immediately adjacent to a special event area identified in and for use in 
conjunction with a Class C per diem municipal to-go BWL license. The board must 
establish regulations for the license and permit, as specified. The fee for the Class C 
per diem municipal to-go BWL license is $45 per day, and the fee for the temporary to‑go 
event permit is $20 per day. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. 
  
Local Effect:  Worcester County revenues increase, beginning in FY 2027, to the extent 
Class C per diem municipal to‑go BWL licenses and temporary to-go event permits are 
issued. Worcester County can handle the monitoring of any additional licenses or permits 
issued using existing resources. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Minimal.

Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:   
 
Class C Per Diem Municipal To-go License  
 
The board must adopt regulations related to the issuance of and requirements for a 
Class C per diem municipal to-go BWL license, including regulations related to (1) the 
designation of a special event area; (2) the hours of sale; (3) age verification requirements; 
(4) the use of specified to-go cups; (5) advertising, posting of notice, and public hearing 
requirements for the license and any associated temporary to-go event permit; and 
(6) public safety and event security requirements. 
 
Temporary To-go Event Permit 
 
The temporary to-go event permit authorizes the holder to sell at retail beer, wine, and 
liquor in approved to-go cups that may be carried outside the licensed establishment.  
 
The board must adopt regulations related to the issuance of and requirements for the permit, 
including regulations related to (1) the designation of a special event area; (2) the hours of 
sale; (3) age verification requirements; (4) the use of specified to-go cups; and 
(5) advertising, posting of notice, and public hearing requirements for the temporary  
to-go event permit. 
 
Current Law:  Generally, the Worcester County Board of License Commissioners is 
authorized to issue alcoholic beverages licenses to retailers that meet specified criteria. 
Class B licenses authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages for restaurants, and Class D 
licenses authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages for taverns. 
 
Local Fiscal Effect:  Worcester County revenues increase, beginning in fiscal 2027, to the 
extent the board issues Class C per diem municipal to-go BWL licenses and temporary 
to‑go event permits. The number of per diem municipal to-go BWL licenses that will be 
issued under the bill is unknown, and any related permits are dependent on where the 
license is issued and if any Class B or Class D license holders choose to apply for a permit. 
Therefore, any potential increase in Worcester County license and permit fee revenues 
under the bill cannot be reliably estimated. Worcester County can handle the bill’s 
requirements with existing resources.  
 
Additional Comments:  There are four municipalities in Worcester County:  Ocean City, 
Pocomoke City, the Town of Berlin, and the Town of Snow Hill. The bill does not authorize 
the board to issue a Class C per diem municipal to-go license to Ocean City.
