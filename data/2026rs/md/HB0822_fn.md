Circuit Court Clerks and Registers of Wills - Maximum Salary 
 
 
This bill alters the maximum annual salary that the Board of Public Works (BPW) may set 
for the clerk of each circuit court and a register of wills from $146,500 to the salary of a 
State employee at Grade 26, Step 20 of the State of Maryland Standard and Bargaining 
Salary Scale. The bill also repeals an obsolete provision establishing a minimum annual 
salary of $12,000 for the register of wills in Baltimore City. Pursuant to constitutional 
requirements, the salary increases for the clerks of the circuit court and the registers 
of wills take effect at the beginning of the next term of office. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  General fund expenditures increase for the Judiciary by $403,800 in FY 2027 
due to the impact of salary increases for the clerks of the court that take effect  
December 1, 2026, as discussed below. Future year expenditures reflect annualization. The 
FY 2027 budget as introduced includes $651,929 for the salary increases. General fund 
revenues decrease by $386,300 in FY 2027 due to the impact of salary increases for the 
registers of wills that take effect December 1, 2026, as discussed below. Future year 
revenue decreases reflect annualization.

Analysis 
 
Current Law: 
 
Clerk of the Circuit Court and Register of Wills 
 
The clerk of each circuit court and the register of wills of each county is entitled to receive 
an annual salary of no more than $124,500. While the permissible range (or maximum) of 
salaries is set by statute, BPW determines the annual salary of: 
 
 
each clerk on the basis of the relative volume of business and receipts in the clerk’s 
office; and 
 
each register of wills in consideration of (1) the population of the county determined 
by the last official U.S. census; (2) the dollar volume of total fees and taxes collected 
and excess fees turned over to the State for each of the preceding five years by the 
office of the register for which the salary is being fixed; and (3) other pertinent data, 
which have relation to the reasonableness of the salary in relation to the work done 
and volume handled by the office. 
 
Article III, § 35 of the Maryland Constitution prohibits the salary or compensation of any 
public officer from being increased or diminished during the individual’s term of office, 
except those whose full term is fixed by law in excess of four years. Clerks of the court and 
registers of wills are elected to four-year terms. 
 
Background: 
 
Clerks of the Circuit Court 
 
The clerk of the circuit court in each jurisdiction is responsible for the custody of records 
and other court documents. Other duties include issuing writs, recording of land 
instruments and other documents, issuing licenses, administering oaths of office, and 
handling matters related to the operation of the court, including the submission of an annual 
budget for the review and approval of the Chief Judge of the Supreme Court of Maryland. 
 
Chapters 188 and 189 of 2022 increased the maximum salary of the clerks of the 
circuit court from $124,500 to $146,500, an 18% increase. Exhibit 1 sets forth the maximum 
annual salary levels since 1982.

Registers of Wills 
 
In general, the registers of wills in each of the State’s 24 jurisdictions are responsible for 
the administration of estates by providing proper forms and direction, assisting and 
advising any person who requests assistance in the preparation of any form for 
administrative probate, admitting wills to probate and issuing letters of administration, 
auditing accounts, and maintaining accurate records of all estate matters. The registers also 
serve as clerks of the orphans’ courts. 
 
Chapter 427 of 2022 increased the maximum salary of the registers of wills from $124,500 
to $146,500, an 18% increase. Exhibit 2 sets forth the maximum annual salary levels since 
1986.

State Revenues:  Registers of wills’ salaries and office expenses are paid from fees and 
inheritance tax revenue collected by the registers, with the remainder of the fees and 
inheritance tax revenue deposited into the general fund. General fund revenues decrease 
by $386,250 in fiscal 2027, which accounts for the registers of wills beginning the next 
term on December 1, 2026, and by approximately $662,142 annually thereafter. This 
analysis, which accounts for fringe benefits, assumes that BPW increases to grade 26, 
step 20 of the State salary scale ($172,463 in fiscal 2027), the salaries of the registers of 
wills who earn the current maximum of $146,500, and increases the salaries of the other 
registers of wills by the same 18%. 
 
State Expenditures:  General fund expenditures for the Judiciary increase by $403,827 in 
fiscal 2027, which accounts for the clerks beginning the next term on December 1, 2026, 
and by approximately $692,274 annually thereafter. This analysis, which accounts for 
fringe benefits, assumes that BPW increases to grade 26, step 20 of the State salary scale, 
($172,463 in fiscal 2027), the salaries of the clerks who earn the current maximum of 
$146,500, and increases the salaries of the other clerks by the same 18%. The FY 2027 
budget as introduced includes $651,929 for the salary increases.
