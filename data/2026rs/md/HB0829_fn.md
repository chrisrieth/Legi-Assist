Public Utilities - For-Hire Drivers and Transportation Network Operators - 
Human Trafficking Awareness and Prevention Training and Liability 
 
 
This bill requires an applicant for a local taxicab driver’s license or State for-hire driver’s 
license to include in the license application documentation verifying that the applicant 
completed a human trafficking awareness training program, as specified. The bill also 
prohibits a transportation network company (TNC) from allowing a transportation network 
operator (TNO) licensee or license applicant to provide transportation network services 
unless the TNO has completed a human trafficking awareness program, as specified. The 
bill further specifies that an applicant for a TNO license or a licensed taxicab driver, 
for-hire driver, or TNO may not be held civilly or criminally liable for reporting or 
responding in good faith to a suspected incident of human trafficking. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  Special fund expenditures for the Public Service Commission (PSC) increase 
by $10,000 in FY 2027 only. Special fund revenues increase correspondingly from 
assessments imposed on public service companies.

Bill Summary: 
 
Licensure Requirements 
 
The following requirements apply to an applicant for a (1) permanent or temporary for-hire 
driver’s license from PSC, including for a license renewal and (2) taxicab driver’s license 
from a county or municipality. Any such applicant must include in the application 
documentation verifying that the applicant completed the U.S. Department of 
Transportation’s (DOT) Leaders Against Human Trafficking/Combatting Human 
Trafficking in the Transportation Awareness Training [sic] or any nationally recognized 
human trafficking awareness training program.  
 
A human trafficking awareness training that an applicant completes under this provision 
must include information on how to (1) recognize potential victims of human trafficking; 
(2) respond to an individual who may be or is a victim of human trafficking; and (3) connect 
an individual who may be or is a victim of human trafficking with available resources.  
 
Transportation Network Operator Requirements 
 
A TNC may not allow an applicant for a TNO license or the holder of a temporary or 
permanent TNO license to provide transportation network services using a digital network 
application unless the applicant or license holder, within 30 days after the applicant or 
license holder first provides a prearranged ride, has completed (1) the U.S. DOT Leaders 
Against Human Trafficking/Combatting Human Trafficking in the Transportation 
Awareness Training [sic]; (2) any nationally recognized human trafficking awareness 
training program; or (3) any digital training developed by a TNC in consultation with a 
nationally recognized organization with expertise in human trafficking awareness training.  
 
A human trafficking awareness training that an applicant completes under this provision 
must include information on how to (1) recognize potential victims of human trafficking; 
(2) respond to an individual who may be or is a victim of human trafficking; and (3) connect 
an individual who may be or is a victim of human trafficking with available resources. 
 
These requirements do not apply to an individual who submitted an application for a TNO 
license by September 30, 2026, and completed, between July 1, 2025, and  
September 30, 2026, a human trafficking awareness training that meets the above 
requirements.

Additional Specifications  
 
The bill makes conforming changes to clarify that completion of the training is generally a 
condition of licensure by: 
 
 
specifying that a local jurisdiction may license taxicab drivers if, at a minimum, the 
local jurisdiction conducts the criminal history and driving record checks required 
by current law and requires each applicant for a license to include in the application 
documentation verifying that the applicant completed the training required by the 
bill; and  
 
explicitly authorizing PSC to approve an applicant and issue a temporary or 
permanent for-hire driver’s license if the applicant meets existing eligibility 
requirements and submits documentation verifying completion of the training 
required by the bill.  
 
Current Law: 
 
State Licensing for Taxicab and For-hire Drivers and Transportation Network Operators  
 
Except for taxicab drivers licensed by a county or municipality and certain individuals who 
provide transportation services in the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit District, a 
person may not operate a motor vehicle for hire in the State unless the person holds a 
for‑hire driver’s license or a TNO license issued by PSC.  
 
Taxicab and For-hire Licensing:  An applicant for a for-hire driver’s license (which 
includes a passenger-for-hire license and a taxicab driver’s license), must submit a 
completed application to PSC on the form provided by PSC, include specified information 
with the application, pay an application fee, and apply to the Criminal Justice Information 
System Central Repository of the Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services 
for a criminal history records check (CHRC).  
 
Generally, PSC may approve an applicant and issue a temporary driver’s license to the 
applicant if (1) all information required for the application is provided and (2) PSC is 
satisfied with the successful submission of an applicant’s national CHRC and driving 
record check, as specified. PSC may issue a permanent for-hire driver’s license to an 
applicant on the submission of a satisfactory supplemental criminal background check; 
however, for taxicab services, the supplemental check must be submitted within 30 days 
of the issuance of a temporary license.

Local Licensing for Certain Taxicab Drivers  
 
A county or municipality may license taxicab drivers who drive taxicabs that are based in 
that county or municipality if, at a minimum, the county or municipality conducts a 
criminal record check and driving record check of each applicant for a license. A taxicab 
driver licensed by a county or municipality is not required to be licensed by PSC.  
 
Human Trafficking Awareness Training  
 
Numerous government entities and licensed businesses in the State are required to provide, 
approve, and/or receive training on the identification and reporting of human trafficking.  
 
For example: 
 
 
the Maryland State Department of Education is required to collaborate with the 
Maryland Department of Health to provide awareness and training for Directors of 
Student Services in local education agencies on human trafficking, including 
strategies for the prevention of trafficking of children;  
 
a commercial driver’s license training school is required to include, as part of its 
curriculum, education and training on the recognition, prevention, and effective 
reporting of human trafficking;  
 
each innkeeper must provide a new employee with an annual training for the 
accurate and prompt identification and reporting of suspected human trafficking 
(any such training program must be approved by the Governor’s Office of Crime 
Prevention and Policy and the Maryland Department of Labor); and 
 
Chapter 437 of 2025 requires the Maryland Department of Transportation to 
develop or identify and implement a human trafficking awareness training program 
and administer the training program to specified employees and contractors.
