A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Local Government – Grant for Recipients of State Child Tax Credit – Authorization

3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a county to provide, by law, a grant to recipients of the State
4 income tax credit for certain dependent children, subject to certain limitations; and generally relating to
5 county grants to recipients of the State income tax credit for dependent children.

21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

23 Article – Local Government

24 12–906.

25 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
26 MEANINGS INDICATED.

27 (2) “FEDERAL ADJUSTED GROSS INCOME”, “FIDUCIARY”,
28 “RESIDENT”, AND “TAXABLE YEAR” HAVE THE MEANINGS STATED IN § 10–101 OF
29 THE TAX – GENERAL ARTICLE.

30 (3) “QUALIFIED CHILD” AND “TAXPAYER” HAVE THE MEANINGS
31 STATED IN § 10–751 OF THE TAX – GENERAL ARTICLE.

32 (B) A COUNTY MAY PROVIDE, BY LAW, FOR A GRANT FOR EACH
33 QUALIFIED CHILD OF A TAXPAYER IF:

34 (1) THE TAXPAYER HAS FEDERAL ADJUSTED GROSS INCOME
35 LOWER THAN THE THRESHOLD AMOUNT OF $15,000; AND

36 (2) THE TAXPAYER AND EACH QUALIFIED CHILD ARE RESIDENTS,
37 OTHER THAN FIDUCIARIES, WHO ON THE LAST DAY OF THE TAXABLE YEAR ARE:

38 (I) DOMICILED IN THAT COUNTY; OR

39 (II) MAINTAIN A PRINCIPAL RESIDENCE OR PLACE OF ABODE
40 IN THAT COUNTY.

41 (C) THE AMOUNT OF A GRANT ALLOWED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE
42 REDUCED BY 10% FOR EACH $1,000, OR FRACTION THEREOF, BY WHICH THE
43 TAXPAYER’S FEDERAL ADJUSTED GROSS INCOME EXCEEDS THE THRESHOLD
44 AMOUNT, EXCEPT THAT THE REDUCTION CANNOT REDUCE THE GRANT BELOW
45 ZERO.

46 (D) A COUNTY MAY PROVIDE, BY LAW, FOR:

47 (1) THE AMOUNT OF THE GRANT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
48 SECTION; AND

49 (2) ANY OTHER PROVISION NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT THE GRANT
50 UNDER THIS SECTION.

51 (E) A COUNTY THAT PROVIDES A GRANT UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL
52 REIMBURSE THE COMPTROLLER FOR REASONABLE EXPENSES INCURRED BY THE
53 COMPTROLLER TO PROVIDE THE COUNTY INFORMATION NECESSARY TO
54 IMPLEMENT THE GRANT.

55 Article – Tax – General

56 10–706.

57 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, a credit allowed under this
58 subtitle is allowed against the State income tax only.

59 (b) A credit under § 10–701 of this subtitle is allowed against the total county and
60 State income taxes.

61 (c) (1) A credit allowed under [§ 10–704(a)(1)] § 10–704(B)(1) or § 10–709(b)(1) of this subtitle is allowed against the State income tax only.

62 (2) A credit allowed under [§ 10–704(a)(2)] § 10–704(B)(2) or § 10–709(b)(2) of this subtitle is allowed against the county income tax only.

1 10–751.

2 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

3 (2) “Qualified child” means a dependent of a taxpayer, if the dependent:

4 (i) is a dependent for purposes of § 152 of the Internal Revenue Code
5 in effect on December 31, 2020; and

6 (ii) 1. is under the age of 6 years; or

7 2. A. is under the age of 17 years; and

8 B. is a child with a disability, as defined under § 8–401 of the
9 Education Article.

10 (3) “Taxpayer” means:

11 (i) an individual filing an income tax return; or

12 (ii) a married couple filing a joint income tax return.

13 (b) A taxpayer who is a resident and has federal adjusted gross income
14 lower than the threshold amount of $15,000 may claim a credit against the State income
15 tax for each qualified child in an amount equal to $500.

16 (c) The amount of the credit shall
17 be reduced by $50 for each $1,000, or fraction thereof, by which the taxpayer’s federal
18 adjusted gross income exceeds the threshold amount, except that the reduction cannot
19 reduce the credit below zero.

20 (d) If the credit allowed under this section in any taxable
21 year exceeds the State income tax for that taxable year, the taxpayer may claim a refund
22 in the amount of the excess.

28 withheld or estimated income tax paid or a refund under § 10–704, § 10–707, [or] § 10–714,
