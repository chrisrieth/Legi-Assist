Professional Licensing Portability - Members of the Foreign Service and Spouses 
 
 
This bill explicitly authorizes a Foreign Service member or a Foreign Service member’s 
spouse who relocates to the State due to an assignment or detail to the State and holds a 
license issued by another jurisdiction to practice under that license for the duration of the 
assignment or detail within Maryland if the individual provides specified documentation 
and satisfies other criteria. If a Foreign Service member or spouse is authorized to practice 
under the bill’s provisions, the individual may practice only in the discipline and scope of 
practice allowed by the license in the other jurisdiction, and the licensing authority in the 
State may not require another license unless the individual seeks to practice outside of the 
authorized discipline or scope of practice. The bill also makes conforming changes.

Bill Summary/Current Law:  
 
Authorization to Practice Under the Bill for Foreign Service Members and Spouses 
 
Definitions:  “Member of the Foreign Service” means an individual who is employed in 
the Foreign Service of the United States. “Licensing authority” means an agency of the 
State that issues a license. “License” means a license, permit, certification, registration, or 
other legal authorization required for engaging in an occupation or a profession. 
 
Application Process:  A member of the Foreign Service or spouse must: 
 
 
provide the licensing authority with a copy of the notification of assignment or 
detail;  
 
remain in good standing with (1) the regulatory authority that issued the initial 
license and (2) all jurisdictions in which the applicant holds or has held a license of 
similar scope of practice and in the same discipline; 
 
have actively used the license within the two years immediately preceding 
relocation; and 
 
comply with the standards of practice, discipline, and fulfillment of any continuing 
education requirements required by the licensing authority in Maryland. 
 
Authorizations and Licensure – In General 
 
Generally, under current law, an individual must be licensed by the appropriate licensing 
authority before practicing specified occupations in the State. For certain occupations, there 
are pathways to practicing in Maryland more readily, including through interstate 
compacts, licensure by reciprocity, and expedited licensure for veterans. 
 
Licensing compacts are legally binding agreements between two or more states that allow 
providers licensed in one state to practice in another state without requiring an additional 
license. As of November 2025, the State has entered eight health occupations licensing 
compacts:  the Audiology and Speech-Language Pathology Interstate Compact; the Nurse 
Licensure Compact; the Interstate Occupational Therapy Licensure Compact; the Interstate 
Physical Therapy Licensure Compact; the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact; the 
Counseling Compact; the Psychology Interjurisdictional Compact; and the Social Work 
Licensure Compact. 
 
Section 1-704 of the Health Occupations Article requires each health occupations board to 
expedite the licensure, certification, or registration of an active service member, eligible

The bill does not apply to an occupation or profession for which an individual may practice 
in the State under an interstate compact or the practice of law.  
 
Supervisory Duties 
 
For certain occupations, under current law, a student, apprentice, or trainee can practice 
under supervision from a licensed individual. The bill alters references to practicing under 
the supervision of a licensed individual to allow students, apprentices, or trainees to 
practice under the supervision of an individual authorized to practice in the State.

Miscellaneous 
 
Under current law, the Automotive Safety Enforcement Division of the Department of 
State Police must administer an examination to each inspection mechanic applicant. The 
bill alters that provision such that an individual authorized to conduct vehicle inspections 
under the bill must not be required to take the examination.   
 
Servicemembers Civil Relief Act 
 
Recent changes to the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) allow for professional 
license portability for Foreign Service members and the spouses of Foreign Service 
members. In general, the provisions in the federal law regarding the application process 
mirror the procedures established in the bill.  
 
State Fiscal Effect:  As discussed below, certain licensing authorities in the State 
anticipate additional expenditures for programming changes to implement the bill, in 
addition to potential reduced revenues from licensing fees. The Department of Legislative 
Services advises, however, that because many of the changes in the bill codify SCRA, some 
of these fiscal effects may not be directly attributable to the bill, but instead to federal 
requirements.  
 
Maryland Department of Agriculture 
 
The Maryland Department of Agriculture advises that most of the licenses for which the 
bill applies already have no-fee interstate reciprocity, with the exception of veterinarians. 
The State Board of Veterinary Medical Examiners (SBVME) estimates that no more than 
10 Foreign Service members and/or spouses will seek licensure as a veterinarian under the 
bill, and current license fees are $300 annually. Therefore, special fund revenues may 
decrease by up to $3,000 on an annual basis. 
 
SBVME advises that the bill may require modifications to its licensure software to 
accommodate the new authorization. Based on an estimate from similar projects, special 
fund expenditures may increase by at least $15,000 in fiscal 2027 for a one-time software 
update.

Maryland State Department of Education 
 
The Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE) advises that it requires updates to 
the teacher licensure platform to create an alternate authorization process for Foreign 
Service members and their spouses eligible under the bill; thus, MSDE general fund 
expenditures may increase by up to $30,000 in fiscal 2027 only. 
 
Judiciary 
 
The Judiciary advises that certain business licenses are issued by the clerks of the circuit 
courts, with license fee revenues subsequently distributed to the general fund and the 
municipal corporation/county where the licensed business is located, as further specified 
in statute. To the extent that licensing fees are not collected due to the bill’s authorization 
process, State general fund revenues and revenues to local jurisdictions are reduced. 
 
Other Agencies 
 
Other agencies impacted by the bill’s changes generally anticipate being able to implement 
the bill using existing budgeted resources. State revenues associated with other licenses are 
anticipated to decrease, likely only minimally, to the extent that individuals take advantage 
of the special procedures under the bill to practice under licenses issued in other 
jurisdictions. 
 
Small Business Effect: Some Foreign Service members and their spouses who require 
professional licenses to work for certain small businesses in the State may benefit from the 
authorization process under the bill. Small business employers may similarly benefit. 
However, any meaningful benefit is likely attributable to the federal changes to SCRA.  
 
Additional Comments:  As of September 2024, there are an estimated 14,400 Foreign 
Service employees in the United States.
