Ways and Means 
 
 
Howard County - Board of Education - Mandatory School Holidays  
Ho. Co. 11-26 
 
 
This bill requires the Howard County Board of Education to close public schools in the 
county for student attendance, starting in the 2027-2028 school year, in observance of 
Yom Kippur and Juneteenth, as well as the first day of Diwali, Eid Al-Adha, Eid Al-Fitr, 
Lunar New Year, and Rosh Hashanah. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The bill only affects the Howard County public school system. 
 
Local Effect:  The bill is not expected to materially affect Howard County school finances, 
as most of the listed holidays are already noninstructional and professional development 
days, with no student attendance required. However, costs may rise to the extent that the 
local school system needs to pay contractual staff for any instructional days added to the 
school calendar. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Public schools must be open for at least 180 days and 1,080 school hours 
at elementary and middle schools or 1,170 hours at high schools during a 10-month period. 
Schools must be open a minimum of 3 hours during each school day, and cannot be open 
on Saturdays, Sundays, or holidays to meet the day or hour requirements.
