1 AN ACT concerning

2 Physicians – Licensing – Physicians and Licensed Physicians Residing in 
3 Other Jurisdictions

4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the State Board of Physicians to issue a certain license to
5 practice medicine to an internationally trained physician under certain
6 circumstances; altering the circumstances under which a physician licensed by and
7 residing in another jurisdiction may practice medicine in the State without a
8 license; and generally relating to the licensure of physicians.

59 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
60 MEANINGS INDICATED.

61 (2) “COMMUNITY SPONSOR” MAY INCLUDE:

62 (I) A FEDERALLY QUALIFIED HEALTH CENTER;

63 (II) A RURAL HEALTH CLINIC;

64 (III) A COMMUNITY HEALTH CENTER;

65 (IV) A GROUP PRACTICE WITH AT LEAST TWO LICENSED
66 PHYSICIANS IN GOOD STANDING WITH THE BOARD;

67 (V) A LOCAL HEALTH DEPARTMENT; AND

68 (VI) A NONPROFIT CLINIC SERVING UNDERSERVED
69 POPULATIONS.

70 (3) “FACILITY” MEANS:

71 (I) A HEALTH SYSTEM, HOSPITAL, HOSPITAL–BASED
72 FACILITY, FREESTANDING EMERGENCY FACILITY, OR URGENT CARE CLINIC
73 THAT:

74 1. HAS AN ACCREDITATION COUNCIL FOR
75 GRADUATE MEDICAL EDUCATION OR AMERICAN OSTEOPATHIC ASSOCIATION
76 RESIDENCY PROGRAM; OR

77 2. IS AFFILIATED WITH THE ACCREDITATION
78 COUNCIL FOR GRADUATE MEDICAL EDUCATION OR THE AMERICAN
79 OSTEOPATHIC ASSOCIATION;

80 (II) A SCHOOL OF MEDICINE;

81 (III) THE NATIONAL INSTITUTES FOR HEALTH; OR

82 (IV) A FEDERALLY QUALIFIED HEALTH CENTER.

83 (4) “FOREIGN COUNTRY” MEANS A COUNTRY OTHER THAN THE
84 UNITED STATES, ITS TERRITORIES OR POSSESSIONS, OR CANADA.

85 (5) “SOLO PRACTICE” MEANS PRIVATE, INDEPENDENT PRACTICE
86 IN WHICH A PHYSICIAN OPERATES WITHOUT PARTNERS OR EMPLOYMENT
87 AFFILIATIONS WITH OTHER ORGANIZATIONS.

88 (6) “STATE STANDARD OF CARE ASSESSMENT” MEANS AN EXAM
89 APPROVED BY THE BOARD TO ASSESS CLINICAL JUDGMENT, MANAGEMENT OF
90 COMPLICATIONS, UNDERSTANDING OF APPROPRIATE COLLABORATION AND
91 REFERRAL PRACTICES, AND ETHICAL STANDARDS, AND DEMONSTRATING THE
92 ABILITY TO CONSTRUCT AND EXECUTE SAFE, APPROPRIATE TREATMENT PLANS,
93 INCLUDING ORAL THEORY AND PRACTICE SESSIONS COVERING:

94 (I) UNEXPECTED CLINICAL SCENARIOS; AND

95 (II) ONCE ANNUAL REVIEW OF A SUBSET OF THE LICENSEE’S
96 ACTUAL CASES BY AN EXAMINER APPROVED BY THE BOARD.

97 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:

98 (1) A PHYSICIAN WHO HAS COMPLETED:

99 (I) ACCREDITATION COUNCIL FOR GRADUATE MEDICAL
100 EDUCATION ACCREDITED RESIDENCY TRAINING IN THE UNITED STATES; OR

101 (II) AMERICAN OSTEOPATHIC ASSOCIATION RESIDENCY
102 TRAINING OR ROYAL COLLEGE OF PHYSICIANS TRAINING IN CANADA; OR

103 (2) A PHYSICIAN WHO HAS PREVIOUSLY RESIDED IN OR HELD A
104 MEDICAL LICENSE FROM THE UNITED STATES, ITS TERRITORIES OR
105 POSSESSIONS, OR CANADA.

106 (C) (1) BEGINNING JANUARY 1, 2028, THE BOARD MAY ISSUE A LIMITED
107 LICENSE TO PRACTICE MEDICINE TO A PHYSICIAN LICENSED IN A COUNTRY
108 OTHER THAN THE UNITED STATES, ITS TERRITORIES OR POSSESSIONS, OR CANADA 
109 WHO MEETS THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.

110 (2) THE TERM OF A LIMITED LICENSE ISSUED BY THE BOARD UNDER THIS
111 SECTION MAY NOT EXCEED 3 YEARS.

112 (3) A LIMITED LICENSE ISSUED BY THE BOARD UNDER THIS SECTION MAY NOT
113 BE RENEWED.
