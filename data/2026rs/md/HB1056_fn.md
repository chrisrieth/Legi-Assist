General Provisions - Commemorative Days - Purple Lights Night 
 
 
This bill requires the Governor to annually proclaim October 1 as Purple Lights Night to 
honor the individuals in the State who are victims or survivors of domestic violence and 
express support for the elimination of domestic violence in the State. The bill takes effect 
July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. Commemorating Purple Lights Night does not affect State finances. 
 
Local Effect:  None. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None.  
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Twenty official commemorative days, seven months, and one week are 
recognized in State law, as shown in Appendix – Official Commemorative Days and 
Months in Maryland. The Governor also must issue a proclamation each year 
encouraging citizens and other individuals to observe a moment of silence at 3:00 p.m. on 
Memorial Day to unite in remembrance and commemorate the heroic acts and efforts of 
Marylanders who have served and died in the U.S. Armed Forces.
