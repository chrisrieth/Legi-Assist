Land Use - Preservation of Natural Resources 
 
 
This bill – under Title 7, Subtitle 1 (Development Mechanisms) of the Land Use Article – 
authorizes and encourages a local jurisdiction to enact local laws providing for or requiring 
the preservation of natural resources through (1) the transfer of development rights; 
(2) conservation or preservation easements; (3) open space or agricultural land preservation 
programs; (4) cluster or conservation subdivision design; (5) riparian buffer, floodplain, or 
wetland protection requirements; and (6) other land use or zoning mechanisms designed to 
provide long-term or permanent protection of natural resources. “Natural resource” 
includes land, water, forests, wetlands, floodplains, habitat areas, sensitive areas, and other 
areas that provide ecological, hydrological, or conservation value.

The authority granted under Title 7, Subtitle 1, is not intended to limit a local jurisdiction’s

(2) adopt other methods to facilitate orderly development and growth, encourage the 
preservation of natural resources, or provide affordable housing. 
 
Transfer of Development Rights 
 
Title 7, Subtitle 2 (Transfer of Development Rights) of the Land Use Article authorizes a 
legislative body to establish a program for the transfer of development rights to 
(1) encourage the preservation of natural resources and (2) facilitate orderly growth and 
development in the State in conjunction with programs for preservation of open space and 
agricultural land and other development management programs and techniques.
