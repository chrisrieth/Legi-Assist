Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise Fund - Establishment ( 
Charles J. Otto Agricultural Education Promise Act) 
 
 
This bill establishes the Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise Fund to 
advance agricultural fairs and education in the State. The bill requires the Governor to 
include $200,000 for the new special fund in the annual budget bill each fiscal year. The 
bill also repeals a requirement that a specified amount from the State horse racing special 
fund be provided to the Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation to promote statewide 
agricultural education. The bill takes effect June 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  No effect in FY 2026. Horse racing special fund revenues increase by 
$75,000 annually beginning in FY 2027, but spending from that fund is unaffected. General 
fund expenditures increase by $200,000 annually beginning in FY 2028, and special fund 
revenues and expenditures increase by the same amount. This bill eliminates a mandated 
distribution beginning in FY 2027 and establishes a mandated appropriation and 
mandated distributions beginning in FY 2028.

Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  The bill establishes the Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise 
Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund, administered by the Comptroller, to advance 
agricultural fairs and education in the State. The fund consists of (1) an annual $200,000 
mandated appropriation included in the State budget; (2) interest earnings; and (3) any 
other money from any other source accepted for the benefit of the fund. 
 
The bill requires the Comptroller to pay annual grants from the fund of (1) $100,000 to the 
Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation to promote and enhance statewide 
agricultural education and (2) $100,000 to the Maryland FFA Association for agricultural 
education and career development.  
 
The bill also repeals a provision of law that mandates a grant from the horse racing  
special fund of $75,000 to the Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation to promote 
and enhance statewide agricultural education. 
 
Current Law:  Revenues from pari-mutuel taxes and license fees, as well as other horse 
racing revenues, are credited to the horse racing special fund. The Comptroller makes the 
following grant distributions from the special fund: 
 
 
$825,000 to the Maryland Agricultural Fair Board (for promotion of fairs and 
exhibits); 
 
$500,000 for the Maryland Million and $350,000 for the Sire Stakes Program; 
 
for as long as the Maryland State Fair remains at the Timonium Fairgrounds, 
$500,000 to the Maryland State Fair and Agricultural Society, Inc. (for promotion 
and enhancement of the fair); 
 
$50,000 to Baltimore County (to replace former revenues); 
 
$100,000 to Prince George’s County (to replace former revenues from the 
admissions and amusement tax); 
 
$40,000 to the Great Frederick Fair to support exhibition harness races; 
 
$75,000 to the Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation; 
 
$20,000 to the Great Pocomoke Fair; 
 
up to $500,000 for the Maryland International Thoroughbred Race; and 
 
local impact aid. 
 
In recent years, revenues from the horse racing special fund have been insufficient to fulfill 
the expected local impact aid allocation to each jurisdiction and the other mandated uses, 
mainly due to a reduction in racing days. In any fiscal year that revenues to the special fund 
are not sufficient to fully fund local impact aid, the Comptroller must proportionately 
reduce the amount of grants required to be paid.

State Fiscal Effect:  General fund expenditures for the Maryland Fair and Agricultural 
Education Promise Fund increase by $200,000 annually beginning in fiscal 2028, and 
special fund revenues for the new fund increase by the same amount beginning in 
fiscal 2028. Special fund expenditures from the new fund to provide grants to the Maryland 
Agricultural Education Foundation and the Maryland FFA Association increase by 
$200,000 annually beginning in fiscal 2028. 
 
The bill repeals a mandated grant from the horse racing special fund of $75,000 to the 
Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation. Thus, $75,000 that would otherwise be 
distributed to the foundation will instead be available to use for other purposes beginning 
in fiscal 2027, all of which continues to be expended. As a result, certain other grantees 
likely receive more than they otherwise would due to the requirement for proportional 
reductions when funding is not sufficient. 
 
Under the bill, the Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation does not receive funding 
in fiscal 2027, but beginning in fiscal 2028 its funding is $100,000 annually.
