Ways and Means 
 
 
County Boards of Education - Appointed Members in Leadership Positions - 
Prohibition 
 
 
This bill prohibits an appointed member to a local board of education from serving in a 
leadership position on the board. An appointed board member serving in a leadership 
position on July 1, 2026, must vacate the leadership position by August 1, 2026. A 
leadership position is defined as a president, vice president, chair, or vice chair. An 
appointed member does not include a member serving on the local board who is appointed 
to fill a vacancy for an elected position on the board. 
 
Failure to comply with the bill’s provisions can result in a reduction in State funding to the 
local board. The Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE) is authorized to refer a 
local board’s noncompliance to the Attorney General for enforcement. MSDE may also 
impose a civil penalty of up to $2,500 on an appointed board member who knowingly and 
willfully continues to serve in a leadership position in violation of the bill’s provisions. 
The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. Enforcement of the bill can be handled with existing resources. 
  
Local Effect:  The local boards of education in four jurisdictions (Baltimore City and 
Baltimore, Caroline, and Harford counties) have hybrid boards with both appointed and 
elected members. The governance and operation of these local school systems may be 
affected. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None.

Appendix – Local Boards of Education Membership 
 
 
The composition of the local boards of education varies with members serving three- to 
four-year terms. Twenty counties have elected school boards and four counties have 
combined appointed and elected school boards. All 24 boards have either student members 
or student representatives who regularly present at board meetings; however, only 8 boards 
allow student members to vote, which generally excludes authority to vote on matters 
relating to collective bargaining and personnel. Three counties with voting student 
members also preclude student members from voting on matters relating to operating and 
capital budgets. Exhibit 1 shows the selection methods, terms of office, and membership 
of the 24 local school boards.
