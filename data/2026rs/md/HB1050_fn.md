Ways and Means 
 
 
State Department of Education - Organ Donation - Development of Educational 
Materials 
 
   
This bill requires the Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE), if it develops, 
updates, or approves curriculum guidance, instructional resources, or professional 
development materials relating to organ, eye, and tissue donation, to consult with the 
qualified nonprofit entity that operates the donor registry and procurement organizations 
serving in Maryland. MSDE may use or reference existing materials developed by those 
entities if the materials are age appropriate; evidence-based and medically accurate; and 
consistent with the Maryland Revised Uniform Anatomical Gift Act. The bill takes effect 
July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  No material effect on State operations or finances. The bill requires 
consultation only if MSDE elects to develop, update, or approve specified materials, and 
MSDE advises the work can be absorbed within existing resources. 
  
Local Effect:  No effect on local school system expenditures or revenues is anticipated. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  “Donor registry” is defined as a database that contains records of 
anatomical gifts and amendments to or revocations of anatomical gifts as provided in 
current law. “Organ procurement organization” means a person designated by the Secretary 
of the United States Department of Health and Human Services as an organ procurement

Maryland Revised Uniform Anatomical Gift Act 
 
The Maryland Revised Uniform Anatomical Gift Act governs donations of all or part of a 
human body (that take effect after the donor’s death) for the purpose of transplantation, 
therapy, research, training, or education. 
 
Among other things, the Act specifies:   
 
 
the classes of individuals, in order of priority, who may make an anatomical gift of 
a donor’s body or part after the donor’s death under certain circumstances;  
 
how an anatomical gift may be revoked or amended and how an individual may 
indicate a refusal to make an anatomical gift of the individual’s body or part;  
 
the relationship between, and priorities of, actions taken by the donor and other 
authorized persons, with respect to making, amending, revoking, or refusing an 
anatomical gift; and  
 
the resolution of conflicts between an advance directive and an anatomical gift.
