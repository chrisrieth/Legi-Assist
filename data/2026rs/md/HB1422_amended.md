A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 State Procurement and Personnel – Liquidated Damages Documentation
2 Requirements and State Fiscal Leadership Capacity

3 FOR the purpose of requiring units of State government to provide certain
4 documentation to the Board of Public Works and the Joint Audit and Evaluation
5 Committee if the unit decides not to pursue certain liquidated damages; authorizing
6 the Secretary of Budget and Management to set the pay scale for certain chief
7 financial officer positions in order to assist with recruitment and retention;
8 requiring the Secretary to establish certain minimum qualifications for chief
9 financial officers to consider certain qualifications; requiring the Department of
10 Budget and Management to adopt certain regulations on or before a certain date;
11 requiring certain chief financial officers to provide to the Office of the
12 Comptroller certain financial information at the close of the fiscal year for a certain
13 report; requiring certain chief financial officers to provide the General Accounting
14 Division of the Office of the Comptroller with certain information and a certain
15 letter; requiring certain units of State government to liquidated damages
16 documentation requirements and take certain actions if the unit does not intend to
17 pursue certain damages; and generally relating to liquidated damages
18 documentation requirements and the chief financial officers of units in the
19 Executive Branch of State government.

40 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
41 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

42 Article – State Finance and Procurement

43 11–101.

44 (a) In this Division II the following words have the meanings indicated unless:

45 (1) the context clearly requires a different meaning; or

46 (2) a different definition is provided for a particular title or provision.

47 (d) “Board” means the Board of Public Works.

48 (aa) (1) “Unit” means an officer or other entity that is in the Executive
49 Branch of the State government and is authorized by law to enter into a procurement
50 contract.

51 (2) “Unit” does not include:

52 (i) a bistate, multistate, bicounty, or multicounty governmental
53 agency; or

54 (ii) a special tax district, sanitary district, drainage district, soil
55 conservation district, water supply district, or other political subdivision of the State.

56 15–113.

57 (a) In this section, “liquidated damages” means damages in an amount
58 designated by the parties to a contract that the injured party is eligible to collect as
59 compensation for a specific breach of the contract.

60 (d) A unit shall obtain the approval of the head of the unit if the unit decides
61 not to include in a contract a liquidated damages clause specified under § 13–218(a)(4)
62 of this article.

63 (e) If a unit decides not to pursue liquidated damages when a specified
64 breach associated with a liquidated damages provision has occurred, the unit shall:

65 (1) obtain the approval of the decision by the head of the unit;

66 (2) maintain documentation on the reason the unit decided not to pursue
67 liquidated damages; AND

68 (3) PROVIDE DOCUMENTATION TO THE BOARD AND THE JOINT
69 AUDIT AND EVALUATION COMMITTEE ON THE REASON THAT THE UNIT INTENDS
70 NOT TO PURSUE LIQUIDATED DAMAGES.
