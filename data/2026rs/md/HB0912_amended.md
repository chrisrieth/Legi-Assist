A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Local Government – Trap–Neuter–Return Policies for Community Cats –
3 Requirements and Restrictions
4 (Ash’s Law)

5 FOR the purpose of establishing that certain provisions of law prohibiting the abandonment or certain neglect of an animal
6 do not apply to a person who is providing care to a community cat under a certain
7 trap–neuter–return policy; establishing that a local law that establishes a
8 trap–neuter–return policy is subject to certain requirements and restrictions; and
9 generally relating to trap–neuter–return policies for community cats.

10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

22 Article – Criminal Law

10–604.

(a) [A] SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A person may not:

(1) overdrive or overload an animal;

(2) deprive an animal of necessary sustenance;

(3) inflict unnecessary suffering or pain on an animal;

(4) cause, procure, or authorize an act prohibited under item (1), (2), or
(3) of this subsection; or

(5) if the person has charge or custody of an animal, as owner or
otherwise, unnecessarily fail to provide the animal with:

(i) nutritious food in sufficient quantity;

(ii) necessary veterinary care;

(iii) proper drink;

(iv) proper air;

(v) proper space;

(vi) proper shelter; or

(vii) proper protection from the weather.

(b) A PERSON WHO PROVIDES CARE TO A COMMUNITY CAT UNDER A
TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 1–1321 OF THE LOCAL
GOVERNMENT ARTICLE MAY NOT BE DEEMED AS HAVING CHARGE OR CUSTODY
OF THE COMMUNITY CAT UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(5) OF THIS SECTION.

(C) (1) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on
conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 90 days or a fine not exceeding
$1,000 or both.

(2) As a condition of sentencing, the court may order a defendant
convicted of violating this section to:

(i) participate in and pay for psychological counseling; and

(ii) pay, in addition to any other fines and costs, all reasonable
costs incurred in removing, housing, treating, or euthanizing an animal confiscated from
the defendant.

(3) As a condition of probation, the court may prohibit a defendant from
owning, possessing, or residing with an animal.

9 Article – Local Government

10 1–1321.

11 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.

13 (2) (I) “COMMUNITY CAT” MEANS A FREE–ROAMING CAT THAT
14 MAY BE CARED FOR BY ONE OR MORE RESIDENTS OF THE IMMEDIATE AREA WITHIN
15 WHICH THE CAT IS LOCATED.

16 (II) “COMMUNITY CAT” INCLUDES A FERAL CAT.

17 (3) (I) “COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER” MEANS A PERSON WHO
18 PROVIDES CARE TO A COMMUNITY CAT, INCLUDING FOOD, SHELTER, OR MEDICAL
19 CARE, UNDER A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS
20 SECTION.

21 (II) “COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A
22 PERSON WHO IS CONSIDERED THE OWNER, HARBORER, CONTROLLER, OR KEEPER
23 OF A COMMUNITY CAT.

24 (4) “EARTIP” MEANS A PROCEDURE THAT:

25 (I) REMOVES THE DISTAL ONE–QUARTER OF THE LEFT EAR OF
26 A COMMUNITY CAT;

27 (II) IS PERFORMED USING ANESTHESIA UNDER THE
28 SUPERVISION OF A LICENSED VETERINARIAN; AND

29 (III) IS DESIGNED TO IDENTIFY A COMMUNITY CAT AS BEING
30 STERILIZED AND VACCINATED FOR RABIES.

1 (5) (I) “TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN” MEANS TO HUMANELY TRAP,
2 STERILIZE, VACCINATE FOR RABIES, EARTIP, AND RETURN A COMMUNITY CAT TO
3 THE LOCATION WHERE THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED.

4 (II) “TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN” INCLUDES PROVIDING CARE TO
5 A COMMUNITY CAT.

6 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO ALL COUNTIES AND MUNICIPALITIES.

7 (C) A LOCAL LAW THAT ESTABLISHES A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY:

8 (1) MAY NOT REQUIRE ANY REGISTRATION OR CERTIFICATION
9 PROCESS FOR A COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER;

10 (2) SHALL AUTHORIZE THE TRAPPING OF A COMMUNITY CAT TO
11 STERILIZE, VACCINATE FOR RABIES, AND EARTIP THE COMMUNITY CAT IN
12 ACCORDANCE WITH FEDERAL OR STATE LAW;

13 (3) SHALL AUTHORIZE A COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER TO RECLAIM
14 AN IMPOUNDED COMMUNITY CAT WITHOUT PROOF OF OWNERSHIP FOR THE
15 PURPOSE OF:

16 (I) CARRYING OUT A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY; OR

17 (II) RETURNING AN EARTIPPED CAT TO THE LOCATION WHERE
18 THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED;

19 (4) UNLESS VETERINARY CARE IS REQUIRED OR A LOCATION IS DEEMED UNSAFE FOR RETURN OF AN EARTIPPED CAT, SHALL REQUIRE THAT:

20 (I) AN EARTIPPED CAT THAT IS RECEIVED BY A LOCAL SHELTER
21 BE RETURN TO THE LOCATION WHERE THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED; AND

22 (II) AN EARTIPPED CAT THAT IS TRAPPED BE RETURNED
23 ON–SITE; AND

24 (5) SHALL ESTABLISH TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN AS THE PREFERRED
25 DISPOSITION OF AN IMPOUNDED COMMUNITY CAT.

26 (D) THIS SECTION SUPERSEDES AND PREEMPTS ANY LAW, ORDINANCE,
27 POLICY, OR REGULATION OF ANY LOCAL GOVERNMENT BODY, AND ANY POLICY OR
28 RULE OF ANY PERSON OR CORPORATION, THAT PROHIBITS OR RESTRICTS THE
29 PRACTICE OF TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN.
