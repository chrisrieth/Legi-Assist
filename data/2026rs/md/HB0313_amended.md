CHAPTER ______

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Landlord and Tenant – Residential Housing – Rental Applications and Tenant
3 Screening

4 FOR the purpose of requiring a landlord to provide certain notice and documentation to
5 a prospective tenant under certain circumstances; providing that a prospective tenant
6 shall be allowed to dispute and provide evidence regarding certain information
7 contained in a tenant screening report; prohibiting a landlord from taking certain
8 actions relating to a certain court proceeding and record; establishing that a landlord
9 is not liable for certain information included in a tenant screening report under certain
10 circumstances and requiring a landlord to report certain information to a person that
11 produces tenant screening reports; establishing certain requirements for a person that
12 produces tenant screening reports; and generally relating to rental applications and
13 prospective tenant screening.

14 BY adding to
15 Article – Real Property
16 Section 8–222
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

21 Article – Real Property

22 8–222.

23 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
24 INDICATED.

25 (2) “ADVERSE ACTION” MEANS:

26 (I) DENIAL OF A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S RENTAL
27 APPLICATION;

28 (II) CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE OF A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S
29 RENTAL APPLICATION; OR

30 (III) PLACEMENT OF A PROSPECTIVE TENANT ON A WAITING
31 LIST BECAUSE OF SCREENING CRITERIA.

32 (3) (I) “CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE” MEANS THE ACCEPTANCE OF
33 A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S APPLICATION CONTINGENT ON A SUBSEQUENT ACTION
34 OR CONDITION.

35 (II) “CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE” INCLUDES:

36 1. SUBJECTING A PROSPECTIVE TENANT TO AN
37 INCREASED RENTAL RATE;

38 2. REQUIRING AN INCREASED SECURITY DEPOSIT; AND

39 3. REQUIRING A COSIGNATURE.

40 (4) “OUTCOME” INCLUDES A CASE:

41 (I) THAT WAS DISMISSED;

42 (II) THAT WAS CANCELED;

43 (III) THAT WAS REDEEMED; OR

44 (IV) WHERE THE TENANT PREVAILED.

45 (5) (I) “PROCEEDING” MEANS A FAILURE TO PAY RENT
46 PROCEEDING INITIATED BY A LANDLORD UNDER § 8–401 OF THIS TITLE NOT
47 RESULTING IN A JUDGMENT OF POSSESSION IN FAVOR OF THE LANDLORD.

48 (II) “PROCEEDING” INCLUDES THE FILING OF A PETITION BY A
49 LANDLORD FOR THE FAILURE OF A TENANT TO PAY RENT REGARDLESS OF WHETHER
50 A HEARING IS HELD BY THE COURT.

51 (6) (I) “RECORD” MEANS AN OFFICIAL RECORD OF AN ACTIVE,
52 PENDING, OR FINAL COURT PROCEEDING KEPT BY THE CLERK OF THE COURT OR
53 OTHER COURT PERSONNEL.

54 (II) “RECORD” INCLUDES:

55 1. AN INDEX, A DOCKET ENTRY, A PETITION, A
56 MEMORANDUM, A TRANSCRIPTION OF PROCEEDINGS, AN ELECTRONIC RECORDING,
57 AN ORDER, AND A JUDGMENT; AND

58 2. ANY ELECTRONIC INFORMATION ABOUT A
59 PROCEEDING ON A WEBSITE MAINTAINED BY THE MARYLAND JUDICIARY.

60 (7) (I) “SHIELDED RECORD” MEANS A RECORD PROTECTED
61 FROM PUBLIC INSPECTION UNDER § 8–502 OR § 8–503 OF THIS TITLE OR THAT THE
62 COURT HAS OTHERWISE SEALED OR ORDERED INACCESSIBLE TO MEMBERS OF THE
63 PUBLIC.

64 (II) “SHIELDED RECORD” INCLUDES A RECORD SHIELDED,
65 SUPPRESSED, OR SEALED IN ANOTHER JURISDICTION UNDER PROVISIONS
66 EQUIVALENT TO THOSE OF THE STATE.

67 (8) “TENANT SCREENING REPORT” MEANS ANY CONSUMER
68 REPORT, TENANT SCREENING SERVICE PRODUCT, OR SIMILAR INFORMATION USED
69 BY A LANDLORD TO EVALUATE A RENTAL APPLICATION.

70 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT
71 SHALL CREATE A DISCLOSURE FORM THAT INCLUDES:

72 (I) THE INFORMATION INCLUDED IN A TENANT SCREENING
73 REPORT;

74 (II) THE CRITERIA THAT MAY RESULT IN THE DENIAL OR
75 CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE OF A RENTAL APPLICATION;

76 (III) THE NAME AND CONTACT INFORMATION OF ANY CONSUMER
77 REPORTING AGENCY OR TENANT SCREENING SERVICE USED BY THE LANDLORD TO
78 OBTAIN A TENANT SCREENING REPORT; AND

79 (IV) THE MAXIMUM AMOUNT THAT MAY BE CHARGED FOR AN
80 APPLICATION FEE.

81 (2) BEFORE ACCEPTING AN APPLICATION FEE OR A SCREENING FEE,
82 A LANDLORD SHALL PROVIDE THE DISCLOSURE DESCRIBED IN PARAGRAPH (1) OF
83 THIS SUBSECTION TO A PROSPECTIVE TENANT.

84 (C) (1) A LANDLORD MAY NOT TAKE ADVERSE ACTION AGAINST A
85 PROSPECTIVE TENANT UNLESS THE LANDLORD PROVIDES TO THE PROSPECTIVE
86 TENANT:

87 (I) WRITTEN NOTICE:

88 1. STATING THE SPECIFIC REASONS FOR THE ADVERSE
89 ACTION;

90 2. AFFIRMING THE LANDLORD DID NOT KNOWINGLY
91 CONSIDER ANY SHIELDED RECORD;

92 3. AFFIRMING THE LANDLORD DID NOT RELY ON AN
93 UNSHIELDED RECORD THAT IS 3 YEARS OLD OR OLDER IN A TENANT SCREENING
94 REPORT THAT DID NOT PROVIDE REASONABLY AVAILABLE INFORMATION
95 REGARDING THE DISPOSITION OR OUTCOME OF THE CLAIM AGAINST THE
96 PROSPECTIVE TENANT; AND

97 4. IDENTIFYING THE TENANT SCREENING REPORT OR
98 OTHER INFORMATION THE LANDLORD RELIED ON IN CONSIDERING THE
99 PROSPECTIVE TENANT;

100 (II) A COPY OF ANY SCREENING REPORT USED BY THE
101 LANDLORD IN REACHING A DETERMINATION OR INFORMATION SUFFICIENT TO
102 ALLOW THE PROSPECTIVE TENANT TO INDEPENDENTLY OBTAIN A COPY OF THE
103 TENANT SCREENING REPORT; AND

104 (III) ANY OTHER INFORMATION REQUIRED FOR ADVERSE
105 ACTION NOTICE UNDER THE FEDERAL FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT.

106 (2) (I) A PROSPECTIVE TENANT SHALL BE ALLOWED TO DISPUTE
107 AND PROVIDE EVIDENCE REGARDING INACCURATE OR INCOMPLETE INFORMATION
108 CONTAINED IN A TENANT SCREENING REPORT.

109 (II) A LANDLORD THAT USES A SCREENING REPORT SHALL
110 NOTIFY A PROSPECTIVE TENANT OF THE RIGHT TO DISPUTE THE REPORT.

111 (D) (1) A LANDLORD MAY NOT:

112 (I) REQUEST OR REQUIRE A PROSPECTIVE TENANT TO
113 DISCLOSE INFORMATION RELATING TO A SHIELDED RECORD;

114 (II) MAKE AN ORAL OR WRITTEN INQUIRY TO ANY PERSON
115 REGARDING A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S SHIELDED RECORD;

116 (III) 1. SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
117 RELY ON A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S SHIELDED RECORD IN MAKING A
118 DETERMINATION REGARDING A RENTAL APPLICATION; OR

119 2. RELY ON AN UNSHIELDED RECORD THAT IS 3 YEARS
120 OLD OR OLDER IN A TENANT SCREENING REPORT THAT DID NOT PROVIDE
121 REASONABLY AVAILABLE INFORMATION REGARDING THE DISPOSITION OR
122 OUTCOME OF THE CLAIM AGAINST THE PROSPECTIVE TENANT;

123 (IV) CONDITIONALLY ACCEPT A PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S
124 APPLICATION CONTINGENT ON THE PROSPECTIVE TENANT’S DISCLOSURE OF A
125 SHIELDED RECORD;

126 (V) TAKE ADVERSE ACTION BASED ON A PROSPECTIVE
127 TENANT’S REFUSAL TO DISCLOSE A SHIELDED RECORD; OR

128 (VI) TAKE ADVERSE ACTION BASED SOLELY ON AN UNSHIELDED
129 RECORD THAT DOES NOT INCLUDE REASONABLY AVAILABLE INFORMATION
130 INDICATING THE DISPOSITION OR OUTCOME OF THE CLAIM AGAINST THE
131 PROSPECTIVE TENANT.

132 (2) A LANDLORD IS NOT LIABLE FOR A DETERMINATION BASED ON A
133 SHIELDED RECORD IF:

134 (I) THE LANDLORD RELIED IN GOOD FAITH ON A TENANT
135 SCREENING REPORT PRODUCED BY A THIRD PARTY; AND

136 (II) THE LANDLORD DID NOT HAVE ACTUAL KNOWLEDGE THAT
137 THE RECORD WAS SHIELDED.

138 (E) (1) A PERSON THAT PRODUCES TENANT SCREENING REPORTS:

139 (I) SHALL IMPLEMENT AND MAINTAIN REASONABLE
140 PROCEDURES TO PREVENT THE DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION REGARDING A
141 PROCEEDING OR SHIELDED RECORD;

142 (II) MAY NOT DISCLOSE INFORMATION ABOUT A PROCEEDING
143 OR SHIELDED RECORD; AND

144 (III) FOR AN UNSHIELDED RECORD, SHALL CLEARLY AND
145 ACCURATELY INDICATE THE DISPOSITION OR OUTCOME OF THE CLAIM AGAINST THE
146 SUBJECT OF THE TENANT SCREENING REPORT.

147 (2) A PERSON THAT DISCLOSES INFORMATION OF A PROCEEDING OR
148 SHIELDED RECORD SHALL PROVIDE TO ALL PARTIES A CORRECTED TENANT
149 SCREENING REPORT WITH THE IMPROPERLY DISCLOSED INFORMATION REMOVED
150 WITHIN 5 BUSINESS DAYS AFTER LEARNING OF THE DISCLOSURE.

151 (F) A PROSPECTIVE TENANT MAY NOT WAIVE ANY OF THE PROTECTIONS
152 ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION AND ANY ATTEMPTED WAIVER BY A
153 PROSPECTIVE TENANT IS VOID.

154 (G) A LANDLORD THAT VIOLATES SUBSECTION (B), (C), OR (D) OF THIS
155 SECTION IS SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY NOT EXCEEDING $500 FOR EACH
156 VIOLATION.

157 (H) AN INDIVIDUAL INJURED BY A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (B) OR (C)
158 OF THIS SECTION MAY SEEK INJUNCTIVE RELIEF AND A REFUND OF ANY
159 APPLICATION OR SCREENING FEE PAID BY THE INDIVIDUAL.

160 (I) AN INDIVIDUAL INJURED BY A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS
161 SECTION MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR:

162 (1) DAMAGES;

163 (2) A REFUND OF ANY APPLICATION OR SCREENING FEE;

164 (3) INJUNCTIVE AND OTHER EQUITABLE RELIEF; AND

165 (4) REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES AND COURT COSTS.

166 (J) AN INDIVIDUAL INJURED BY A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (E) OF THIS
167 SECTION MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR INJUNCTIVE OR OTHER EQUITABLE RELIEF,
168 INCLUDING REQUIRING A TENANT SCREENING COMPANY TO SEND CORRECTIVE
169 NOTICES.

170 (K) EXCEPT FOR THE PROHIBITION AGAINST THE KNOWING USE OF A
171 SHIELDED RECORD IN REACHING A DETERMINATION UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF
172 THIS SECTION, THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE INTERPRETED TO PROHIBIT A LANDLORD
173 FROM RELYING ON A LAWFULLY OBTAINED CREDIT SCORE OR CONSUMER REPORT
174 SOLELY BECAUSE THE CREDIT SCORE OR CONSUMER REPORT MAY RELATE TO
175 EVICTION ACTIVITY.

176 (L) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT
177 THIS SECTION.

178 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
179 October 1, 2026.
