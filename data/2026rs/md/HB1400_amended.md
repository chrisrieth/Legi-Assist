A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Shellfish Aquaculture – Penalties – Suspension or Revocation of Permit or
3 Registration Card

4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the suspension or revocation of a shellfish aquaculture
5 harvester permit or shellfish aquaculture harvester registration card for certain
6 violations; prohibiting a person whose permit or registration card has been
7 suspended or revoked in accordance with this Act from engaging or working in any
8 aquaculture activity during the suspension or revocation; authorizing a certain
9 leaseholder and the Department of Natural Resources to complete within certain time
10 periods a process to remove gear or transfer a shellfish aquaculture lease in a certain
11 manner; and generally relating to shellfish aquaculture.

12 BY adding to
13 Article – Natural Resources
14 Section 4–1212

16 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

1 (3) “REGISTRATION CARD” MEANS A SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE
2 HARVESTER REGISTRATION CARD ISSUED BY THE DEPARTMENT.

3 (B) (1) IN ADDITION TO ANY OTHER PENALTY OR FINE PROVIDED IN THIS
4 TITLE, A PERSON WHO HOLDS A SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE HARVESTER PERMIT OR
5 SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE HARVESTER REGISTRATION CARD AND RECEIVES A
6 CITATION FOR AN OFFENSE LISTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION
7 MAY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION, HAVE THE PERMIT OR REGISTRATION
8 CARD:

9 (I) FOR A FIRST VIOLATION, SUSPENDED FOR UP TO 5 YEARS;
10 AND

11 (II) FOR A SECOND OR SUBSEQUENT VIOLATION, REVOKED.

12 (2) THE FOLLOWING OFFENSES, COMMITTED IN VIOLATION OF THIS
13 TITLE OR OF ANY REGULATION ADOPTED UNDER THIS TITLE, ARE GROUNDS FOR
14 SUSPENSION OR REVOCATION OF A PERMIT OR REGISTRATION CARD UNDER THIS
15 SECTION:

16 (I) TAKING OYSTERS FROM A CLOSED OR PROHIBITED AREA
17 MORE THAN 200 FEET OUTSIDE A SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE LEASE;

18 (II) DELIVERING OYSTERS TO A DEALER MORE THAN 1 HOUR
19 AFTER THE TIME REQUIRED BY THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH’S VIBRIO
20 PARAHAEMOLYTICUS CONTROL PLAN AS SET FORTH IN COMAR 10.15.07.06;

21 (III) TAKING OYSTERS FROM A LEASED AREA BY A PERSON
22 OTHER THAN THE LEASEHOLDER OR THE LEASEHOLDER’S DESIGNEE;

23 (IV) TAKING OYSTERS FOR COMMERCIAL PURPOSES FROM A
24 NATURAL OYSTER BAR OR A PUBLIC SHELLFISH FISHERY AREA WITHOUT A VALID
25 AUTHORIZATION TO CATCH OYSTERS UNDER § 4–701 OF THIS TITLE; AND

26 (V) UNLAWFULLY TAKING OYSTERS FOR COMMERCIAL
27 PURPOSES FROM A NATURAL OYSTER BAR OR A PUBLIC SHELLFISH FISHERY
28 AREA AND PLANTING THOSE OYSTERS ON A SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE LEASE.

29 (C) (1) BEFORE THE SUSPENSION OR REVOCATION OF A PERMIT OR
30 REGISTRATION CARD UNDER THIS SECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL HOLD A
31 HEARING ON THE MATTER IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE ADMINISTRATIVE
32 PROCEDURE ACT UNDER TITLE 10, SUBTITLE 2 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT
33 ARTICLE.

1 (2) AFTER A HEARING IS CONDUCTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
2 THIS SUBSECTION, IF THE PRESIDING OFFICER FINDS OR CONCLUDES THAT THE
3 PERSON KNOWINGLY HAS COMMITTED AN OFFENSE LISTED UNDER SUBSECTION
4 (B)(2) OF THIS SECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SUSPEND OR REVOKE THE
5 PERSON’S PERMIT OR REGISTRATION CARD IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION
6 (B)(1) OF THIS SECTION.

7 (D) A PERSON WHO IS AGGRIEVED BY THE FINAL DECISION OF THE
8 DEPARTMENT MAY OBTAIN JUDICIAL REVIEW OF THE DECISION IN ACCORDANCE
9 WITH THE ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEDURE ACT UNDER TITLE 10, SUBTITLE 2 OF THE
10 STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.

11 (E) A PERSON WHOSE PERMIT OR REGISTRATION CARD HAS BEEN
12 SUSPENDED OR REVOKED IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION MAY NOT ENGAGE
13 OR WORK IN ANY AQUACULTURE ACTIVITY DURING THE SUSPENSION OR
14 REVOCATION WHETHER OR NOT THE WORK REQUIRES THE USE OF ANOTHER
15 PERMIT OR REGISTRATION CARD.

16 (F) (1) (I) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A PERSON WHO IS
17 THE SOLE HOLDER OF A SHELLFISH AQUACULTURE LEASE UNDER SUBTITLE 11A
18 OF THIS TITLE AND WHOSE PERMIT OR REGISTRATION CARD HAS BEEN
19 SUSPENDED OR REVOKED UNDER THIS SECTION.

20 (II) WITHIN 90 DAYS AFTER THE CONCLUSION OF AN
21 ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEEDING UNDER § 4–11A–10 OF THIS TITLE THAT RESULTS
22 IN THE TERMINATION OF A LEASE, A PERSON MAY PROVIDE TO THE DEPARTMENT
23 A PLAN TO REMOVE ANY GEAR OR OTHER EQUIPMENT FROM THE LEASED AREA
24 OR TRANSFER THE LEASE TO ANOTHER PERSON.

25 (III) WITHIN 60 DAYS AFTER RECEIPT OF THE PLAN UNDER
26 SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL REVIEW
27 THE PLAN AND NOTIFY THE PERSON WHETHER THE PLAN IS COMPLETE OR
28 REQUIRES REVISIONS.

29 (IV) WITHIN 30 DAYS AFTER THE DEPARTMENT’S
30 NOTIFICATION UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (III) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, THE PERSON
31 SHALL SUBMIT ANY REQUIRED PLAN REVISIONS AND A LEASE TRANSFER
32 APPLICATION TO THE DEPARTMENT.

33 (V) WITHIN 90 DAYS AFTER RECEIPT OF THE PERSON’S
34 COMPLETED LEASE TRANSFER APPLICATION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
35 APPROVE THE LEASE TRANSFER.

36 (2) THE DEPARTMENT MAY NOT TERMINATE A PERSON’S LEASE
37 BEFORE THE CONCLUSION OF THE PROCESS UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
38 SUBSECTION.

39 (3) A PERSON’S FAILURE TO COMPLETE THE PROCESS UNDER
40 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION MAY RESULT IN THE FORFEITURE OF ANY
41 GEAR AND EQUIPMENT ON THE LEASED AREA TO THE STATE FOR DESTRUCTION
42 OR DISPOSITION AS THE DEPARTMENT MAY DEEM APPROPRIATE.

43 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
44 October 1, 2026.
