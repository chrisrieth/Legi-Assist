A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Consumer Protection – Retail Transactions for Essential Consumer Goods – Cash Payments

3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a merchant, in a certain transaction, from prohibiting a
4 person from making a cash payment to purchase an essential consumer good, requiring a
5 person to purchase an essential consumer good by using a credit or debit card, or
6 charging or collecting from a person a higher price than for an equivalent cashless transaction for making a
7 cash payment for the purchase of an essential consumer good, subject to certain exceptions;
8 establishing that a violation of this Act is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade
9 practice subject to certain enforcement and penalty provisions under the Maryland
10 Consumer Protection Act; and generally relating to retail cash transactions for essential consumer goods.

3 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:

4 (14) Violation of a provision of:

5 (xlvii) Title 14, Subtitle 50 of this article; [or]

6 (xlviii) Section 13–411.1(c)(2) of the Transportation Article; or

7 (XLIX) SECTION 14–1330 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR

8 14–1330.

9 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
10 INDICATED.

11 (2) “CONSUMER GOODS” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS
12 ARTICLE.

13 (3) “ESSENTIAL CONSUMER GOOD” MEANS:

14 (I) FOOD, INCLUDING HUMAN AND ANIMAL FOOD;

15 (II) WATER, INCLUDING ICE, AND OTHER BEVERAGES;

16 (III) PERSONAL HYGIENE PRODUCTS, INCLUDING TOILETRIES AND
17 CLEANING PRODUCTS;

18 (IV) HEALTH PRODUCTS, INCLUDING MEDICINE, MEDICAL
19 SUPPLIES AND EQUIPMENT, AND PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT; AND

20 (V) FUEL.

21 (4) “MERCHANT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS ARTICLE.

22 (5) “RETAIL ESTABLISHMENT” MEANS AN ESTABLISHMENT THAT SELLS
23 CONSUMER GOODS TO THE PUBLIC.

24 (B) (1) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO AN IN–PERSON TRANSACTION AT A
25 RETAIL ESTABLISHMENT TOTALING LESS THAN $300.

26 (2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:

27 (I) A TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR INTERNET TRANSACTION;

28 (II) A VENDING MACHINE OR KIOSK TRANSACTION; OR

29 (III) A TRANSACTION WITH A MERCHANT THAT SELLS TO THE
30 PUBLIC PRIMARILY:

31 1. FOOD OR BEVERAGES FOR ON–PREMISES
32 CONSUMPTION; OR

33 2. A LIMITED VARIETY OF GOURMET OR SPECIALTY
34 FOODS, INCLUDING CHEESES, FRUITS, NUTS, CANDY, SPICES, AND OILS.

35 (C) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, A
36 MERCHANT MAY NOT:

37 (1) PROHIBIT A PERSON FROM MAKING A CASH PAYMENT TO
38 PURCHASE AN ESSENTIAL CONSUMER GOOD;

39 (2) REQUIRE A PERSON TO USE A CREDIT OR DEBIT CARD TO
40 PURCHASE AN ESSENTIAL CONSUMER GOOD; OR

41 (3) CHARGE OR COLLECT FROM A PERSON A HIGHER PRICE THAN
42 CHARGED OR COLLECTED FOR AN EQUIVALENT CASHLESS TRANSACTION,
43 WHETHER OR NOT THE DIFFERENCE IN PRICE IS DESCRIBED AS A FEE, FOR
44 MAKING A CASH PAYMENT FOR THE PURCHASE OF AN ESSENTIAL CONSUMER
45 GOOD.

46 (D) A MERCHANT MAY PROHIBIT A PERSON FROM MAKING A CASH
47 PAYMENT FOR THE PURCHASE OF A CONSUMER GOOD:

48 (1) IN A CASH DENOMINATION GREATER THAN $20; OR

49 (2) IF THE MERCHANT:

50 (I) PROVIDES AND MAINTAINS A DEVICE ON THE PREMISES
51 OF THE ESTABLISHMENT THAT CONVERTS CASH INTO A PREPAID CARD THAT A
52 PERSON MAY USE TO PURCHASE A CONSUMER GOOD AT THE ESTABLISHMENT;

53 (II) DOES NOT CHARGE ANY FEE OR REQUIRE A DEPOSIT;
54 AND

55 (III) IF THE DEVICE MALFUNCTIONS OR BECOMES
56 INOPERABLE, ACCEPTS PAYMENT IN CASH IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION
57 UNTIL THE DEVICE RESUMES NORMAL OPERATIONS.

20 OF THIS PARAGRAPH:

21 1. ARE CIVIL PENALTIES RECOVERABLE IN
