Municipalities - Election Dates - Reporting Requirements 
 
 
This bill requires a municipality to report the date of each municipal election to the State 
Board of Elections (SBE), at least six months before each regular municipal election and 
within five business days after a special election is scheduled. SBE, each local board of 
elections (with respect to municipal elections in the applicable county), and each 
municipality (that maintains a website) must prominently display a list of all upcoming 
regular and special municipal elections on their respective websites, including the name of 
the municipality (on SBE and local board websites), the election date, and the type of 
election (regular or special election). SBE must also display a list indicating which 
municipalities have not yet reported upcoming regular and special elections. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  The bill is not expected to materially affect State finances. 
  
Local Effect:  The bill is not expected to materially affect local government finances. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  State election law governs the administration of federal, State, county, and 
Baltimore City elections held during statewide elections every two years, and special 
elections, but generally does not govern municipal elections other than those in 
Baltimore City (which is considered a “county” for purposes of State law). Municipal 
elections are primarily governed by each municipality’s law and occur at various times 
throughout the year and in odd- and even-numbered years.

While State law governing elections generally does not apply to municipal elections, there 
are certain provisions of State law that address municipal elections, including provisions 
that (1) require municipalities to submit their election results to SBE to publish on the 
SBE website and (2) allow a municipality to request that SBE include the offices and 
questions to be voted on in a municipal election on the statewide ballot (within the 
municipality) during a statewide election. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.
