A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Correctional Services – Maryland Parole Commission – Members and Hearing
3 Examiners

4 FOR the purpose of requiring a new commissioner to be appointed within a certain
5 number of days under certain circumstances; prohibiting a hearing examiner
6 appointed under certain authority from serving for more than a certain number of
7 days; altering certain requirements for hearing examiners; requiring a vote of the
8 majority of commissioners appointed to the Maryland Parole Commission,
9 instead of a certain minimum number, to approve a certain incarcerated individual
10 for parole; altering the appointing authority and method of appointment of parole
11 commissioners; and generally relating to the Maryland Parole Commission.

114 7–205.

115 (a) The Commission has the exclusive power to:

116 (1) authorize the parole of an individual sentenced under the laws of the
117 State to any correctional facility in the State;

118 (2) negotiate, enter into, and sign predetermined parole release
119 agreements as provided under subsection (b) of this section;

120 (3) hear cases for parole or administrative release in which:

121 (i) the Commissioner of Correction, after reviewing the
122 recommendation of the appropriate managing official, objects to a parole;

123 (ii) the incarcerated individual was convicted of a homicide;

124 (iii) the incarcerated individual is serving a sentence of NOT LESS THAN 15 YEARS CONFINEMENT FOR A SINGLE CONVICTION;

125 (iv) the parole hearing is open to the public under § 7–304 of this title;

126 (v) the incarcerated individual fails to meet the requirements of the
127 administrative release process established under § 7–301.1 of this title;

128 (vi) a victim requests a hearing as provided under § 7–301.1 of this
129 title; or

130 (vii) the Commission finds that a hearing for administrative release
131 is necessary under § 7–301.1 of this title;

132 (4) hear exceptions to recommendations of a hearing examiner or a
133 commissioner acting as a hearing examiner;

134 (5) review summarily all recommendations of a hearing examiner or a
135 commissioner acting as a hearing examiner to which an exception has not been filed;

136 (6) hear a case for parole in absentia when an individual who was
137 sentenced in this State to serve a term of imprisonment is in a correctional facility of a
138 jurisdiction other than this State;

139 (7) hear cases of parole revocation;

140 (8) if delegated by the Governor, hear cases involving an alleged
141 violation of a conditional pardon; and

142 (9) determine conditions for administrative release under § 7–301.1
143 of this title.

144 7–307.

145 (a) (1) Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, the chairperson of
146 the Commission shall assign at least two commissioners to hear cases for parole release as
147 a panel.

148 (2) Each proceeding before a Commission panel shall be conducted in
149 accordance with this section.

150 (b) (1) (i) A Commission panel that consists of two commissioners shall
151 determine, by unanimous vote, whether the incarcerated individual is suitable for parole
152 in accordance with the factors and other information specified in § 7–305 of this subtitle.

153 (ii) If the two–commissioner panel is unable to reach a unanimous
154 decision, the chairperson of the Commission shall convene a three–commissioner panel as
155 soon as practicable to rehear the case.

156 (2) A Commission panel that consists of three commissioners shall
157 determine, by majority vote, whether the incarcerated individual is suitable for parole in
158 accordance with the factors and other information specified in § 7–305 of this subtitle.

159 (c) For an incarcerated individual who has been sentenced to life imprisonment
160 after being convicted of a crime committed on or after October 1, 2021, THE AFFIRMATIVE VOTE OF A MAJORITY OF THE COMMISSIONERS APPOINTED TO THE COMMISSION IS required to approve the incarcerated individual for parole, based on
161 consideration of the factors specified in § 7–305 of this subtitle.

162 (d) (1) The Commission panel shall inform the incarcerated individual and the
163 appropriate correctional authority of the Commission’s decision as soon as possible.

164 (2) If parole is denied, the Commission shall give the incarcerated
165 individual a written report of its findings within 30 days after the hearing.
