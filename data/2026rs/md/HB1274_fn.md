Ways and Means 
 
 
Education - Posthumous Diploma Awards - Authorized 
 
 
This bill authorizes a local school system to award a standard high school diploma, a 
posthumous honorary diploma, or an in memoriam diploma to a deceased student who 
meets specified eligibility criteria. The bill establishes conditions under which each type 
of diploma may be awarded, requires a local school system to determine credit thresholds 
for posthumous standard and honorary diplomas, and requires that a diploma be awarded 
only upon written request of a parent, legal guardian, or next of kin. Posthumous and 
in memoriam diplomas awarded under the bill are ceremonial and do not confer legal rights 
associated with academic completion Any diploma awarded under the bill may not be 
included in graduation rate calculations. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The bill applies only to local school systems. 
  
Local Effect:  Local school system expenditures may increase minimally to process 
requests and issue posthumous diplomas; however, any costs are expected to be absorbable 
within existing resources. Revenues are not affected. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None.

Bill Summary: 
 
Standard High School Diploma (Posthumous) 
 
A local school system may award a standard high school diploma to a deceased student 
who, at the time of death:  (1) was a resident of the county; (2) was enrolled in grade 12 in 
a public school; (3) was within a certain number of credits of completing the credits

Posthumous Honorary Diploma 
 
A local school system may award a posthumous honorary diploma to a deceased student 
who, at the time of death:  (1) was a resident of the county; (2) was enrolled in grade 11 
or 12 in a public school; and (3) was within a certain number of credits of completing 
graduation requirements. A posthumous honorary diploma is ceremonial in nature. 
 
In Memoriam Diploma 
 
A local school system may award an in memoriam diploma to a deceased student who, at 
the time of death:  (1) was a resident of the county; or (2) was enrolled in any grade from 
kindergarten through grade 10 in a public school. An in memoriam diploma is ceremonial 
in nature and recognizes the student’s enrollment and membership in the school 
community. 
 
For both the standard high school diploma and the posthumous honorary diploma, the local 
school system must determine the number of credits required for eligibility. The number 
of credits required for a standard high school diploma must be significantly higher than the 
number required for a posthumous honorary diploma. 
 
Current Law: 
 
High School Diploma Requirements 
 
With the advice of the State Superintendent of Schools, the State Board of Education must 
establish minimum requirements for issuing certificates and diplomas by public and private 
high schools. Local school systems may establish graduation requirements beyond the 
minimum requirements established by the board. 
 
According to regulations, to be awarded a high school diploma, a student entering grade 9 
in the 2021-2022 school year or later must be enrolled in a Maryland public school system 
and have earned a minimum of 22 credits in specified subjects. 
 
Local Fiscal Effect:  Local school system expenditures may increase minimally to 
process requests and verify eligibility for posthumous diplomas. MSDE advises that 
implementation can be managed with existing resources. Several local school systems, 
including Anne Arundel County Public Schools and Wicomico County Public Schools, 
report no fiscal impact, while Frederick County Public Schools advises that any additional 
diploma printing costs are negligible and absorbable within existing resources.
