CHAPTER ______

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Charles J. Otto Agricultural Education Promise Act

3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise
4 Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund to provide grants that advance agricultural fairs
5 and education in the State; requiring interest earnings of the Fund to be credited to
6 the Fund; and generally relating to the Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise Fund.

7 BY adding to
8 Article – Agriculture
9 Section 10–2201 through 10–2203 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 22.
10 Maryland Fair and Agricultural Education Promise Fund”
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Business Regulation
15 Section 11–403
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

20 Article – Agriculture

21 SUBTITLE 22. MARYLAND FAIR AND AGRICULTURAL EDUCATION PROMISE FUND.

22 10–2201.

23 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “FUND” MEANS THE MARYLAND FAIR AND AGRICULTURAL
24 EDUCATION PROMISE FUND.

25 10–2202.

26 (A) THERE IS A MARYLAND FAIR AND AGRICULTURAL EDUCATION
27 PROMISE FUND.

28 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO PROVIDE GRANTS THAT ADVANCE
29 AGRICULTURAL FAIRS AND EDUCATION IN THE STATE.

30 (C) THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.

31 (D) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
32 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

33 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND SEPARATELY,
34 AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.

35 (E) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:

36 (1) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE FUND;

37 (2) INTEREST EARNINGS; AND

38 (3) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED
39 FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.

40 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY:

41 (1) TO PROVIDE ANNUAL GRANTS IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 10–2203
42 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

43 (2) FOR ADMINISTRATIVE EXPENSES OF THE COMPTROLLER IN
44 CARRYING OUT THIS SUBTITLE.

45 (G) (1) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE FUND
46 IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.

47 (2) ANY INTEREST EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED TO
48 THE FUND.

49 (H) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN ACCORDANCE
50 WITH THE STATE BUDGET.

51 (I) FOR EACH FISCAL YEAR, THE GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE
52 ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF $200,000 TO THE FUND.

53 10–2203.

54 EACH YEAR, THE COMPTROLLER SHALL PAY FROM THE FUND AN ANNUAL
55 GRANT OF:

56 (1) $100,000 TO THE MARYLAND AGRICULTURAL
57 EDUCATION FOUNDATION, INC., TO PROMOTE AND ENHANCE STATEWIDE
58 AGRICULTURAL EDUCATION; AND

59 (2) $100,000 TO THE MARYLAND FFA ASSOCIATION
60 FOR AGRICULTURAL EDUCATION AND CAREER DEVELOPMENT.

61 Article – Business Regulation

62 11–403.

63 (a) The Comptroller shall pay from the Special Fund an annual grant of:

64 (1) $825,000 to the Maryland Agricultural Fair Board to promote State
65 and county agricultural fairs and exhibits;

66 (2) $100,000 to Prince George’s County to replace money formerly received
67 from the admissions and amusement tax;

68 (3) $40,000 to the Great Frederick Fair to support exhibition
69 harness racing with money for construction and maintenance of new stalls, track
70 maintenance, and purses;

71 (4) $50 to the City of Bowie for each day that the training facilities
72 are open at the Bowie Race Course Training Center;

73 (5) $75,000 to the Maryland Agricultural Education Foundation, Inc., to
74 promote and enhance statewide agricultural education;

75 (6) an amount not to exceed $30,000 in fiscal year 1998 and $20,000
76 in each fiscal year thereafter to the Great Pocomoke Fair, Inc. to support exhibition harness
77 racing with money for construction and maintenance of new stalls, track maintenance, and
78 purses;

79 (7) $500,000 to the Maryland Million, Ltd. to support and promote
80 the running of Maryland Million races;

81 (8) $350,000 to the Maryland Standardbred Race Fund for the Sire
82 Stakes Program; and

83 (9) beginning July 1, 2017, from the money distributed under §
84 9–120(b) of the State Government Article up to $500,000 to a purse for the Maryland
85 International thoroughbred race under § 11–522.1 of this title.

86 (b) (1) In fiscal year 2017, the Comptroller shall pay, from the money
87 distributed to the Special Fund, $500,000 to the Maryland Racing Commission to be used,
88 in a manner determined by the Maryland Racing Commission, for a bonus award program
89 for Maryland–bred or Maryland–sired horses running in the Preakness Stakes.

90 (2) The Maryland Racing Commission shall consult with representatives of
91 the thoroughbred racing industry prior to establishing the rules and criteria for the bonus
92 award program.

93 (3) If, under the rules of the bonus award program, funds remain in the
94 program after the Preakness Stakes is conducted on one or more occasions, remaining funds
95 shall carry over to the next fiscal year and may not revert to the General Fund.

96 (c) If the Maryland State Fair remains at the Timonium Fair Grounds, the
97 Comptroller shall pay from the Special Fund an annual grant of:

98 (1) $500,000 to the Maryland State Fair and Agricultural Society, Inc., to:

99 (i) promote and enhance the Maryland State Fair; and

100 (ii) maintain and develop youth programs, with premium money
101 provided to organizations, such as 4–H Clubs and the Future Farmers of America, for
102 recognition and awards; and

103 (2) $50,000 to Baltimore County to replace the money formerly received by
104 the county under this subtitle.

105 (d) Any amounts paid by the Comptroller for the purposes specified under
106 subsections (a)(9) and (b) of this section shall remain available for those
107 purposes and may not be used for any other purpose.

108 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
109 1, 2026.
