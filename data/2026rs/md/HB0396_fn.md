Residential Child Care Programs - Education of Children and Training of Child 
and Youth Care Practitioners 
 
   
This bill requires a licensed operator of a residential child care program that receives State 
funding and has legal custody or care and control of a child who is at least five years old 
to provide specified educational opportunities to the child. The bill also establishes 
requirements for training programs for residential child and youth care practitioners 
approved by the State Board for the Certification of Residential Child Care Program 
Professionals. An individual who holds a certificate as a residential child and youth care 
practitioner on July 1, 2026, may not be required to complete (1) any training program 
required by the bill that the individual has already completed and (2) any new training 
program required by the bill until the individual’s certificate is due for renewal. The bill 
takes effect July 1, 2026.  
   
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  The board can expand training program requirements with existing budgeted 
resources. General fund expenditures for the Department of Human Services (DHS) 
increase by $25,000 in FY 2027 only to complete staff training, update regulations, and 
modify contracts to ensure compliance with educational requirements. Future year DHS 
expenditures increase by an indeterminate but likely minimal amount for ongoing 
compliance monitoring. The Maryland Department of Health (MDH) and the Department 
of Juvenile Services (DJS) can ensure compliance with existing budgeted resources. 
Revenues are not affected. 
  
Local Effect:  The bill does not directly affect local governmental operations or finances. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Minimal.

Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  The bill removes the requirements for each licensed operator of a 
residential child care program that receives State funding and has legal custody or care and 
control of a child ages 5 to 15 to (1) enroll the child in the local school system where the 
residential child care program is located unless the program operates an approved 
educational program and (2) expeditiously initiate and monitor the transfer of the academic 
records of a child in the operator’s care from the transferring school to insure that the 
academic records are transferred to the school that the child will be attending while living 
in the operator’s care.  
 
Each licensed operator of a residential child care program that receives State funding and 
has legal custody or care and control of a child who is at least five years old must: 
 
 
ensure the child remains enrolled in the school in which the child was previously 
enrolled, unless a determination is made by a local department of social services 
that remaining enrolled is not in the child’s best interest, then (1) the child must be 
enrolled in the local school system where the residential child care program is 
located or (2) if the residential child care program operates an approved educational 
program in accordance with the licensing regulations that govern residential child 
care programs, the child must be enrolled in that program; 
 
meet the child’s teacher at the time of enrollment and at any other time the school 
or teacher requests to determine the best ways to support the child;  
 
contact the child’s teacher with any questions about school assignments, the child’s 
behavior at school, and extracurricular opportunities to support the child; and  
 
ensure that the child is provided:  (1) regularly scheduled time to study to support 
the child’s academic work; (2) assistance with homework assignments, projects, 
skills practice, and test preparation to reinforce educational concepts taught to the 
child; (3) encouragement and support for participation in extracurricular activities; 
(4) for a child with an individualized education program (IEP) or a 504 plan, 
applicable educational goals to complete within the residential child care program 
that are consistent with the child’s IEP or 504 plan; (5) for a child with a behavioral 
intervention plan (BIP), behavioral supports and goals that reinforce the goals of the 
child’s BIP in the school setting; and (6) for children enrolled in high school or an 
institution of higher education, access to a computer to complete assignments that 
require computer software or the internet.

must include training on: 
 
 
the role of the residential child and youth care practitioner;

Current Law:   
 
Residential Child Care Programs  
 
A residential child care program provides care for children 24 hours a day within a 
structured set of services and activities designed to achieve objectives related to the needs 
of the children served. Residential child care programs are licensed by MDH, DHS, and 
DJS and include group homes, alternative living units, and emergency shelter care.  
 
State Board for the Certification of Residential Child Care Program Professionals 
 
The State Board for the Certification of Residential Child Care Program Professionals is 
the State certification authority for residential child care program administrators and 
residential child and youth care practitioners employed by programs licensed by MDH, 
DHS, and DJS.  
 
To qualify for a certificate as a residential child and youth care practitioner, an applicant 
must successfully complete a training program approved by the board. An applicant who 
has an associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university may be 
waived from the training program requirement, if the applicant passes an examination and 
meets other requirements established by the board.

Residential Child Care Program Education Requirements  
 
Each licensed operator of a residential child care program that receives State funding and 
has legal custody or care and control of a child ages 5 to 15 must: 
 
 
enroll the child in the local school system where the residential child care program 
is located unless the program operates an approved educational program; 
 
expeditiously initiate and monitor the transfer of the academic records of a child in 
the operator’s care from the transferring school to ensure that the academic records 
are transferred to the school that the child will be attending while living in the 
operator’s care; 
 
meet the child’s teachers at the time of enrollment and at any other time the school 
or a teacher requests; and 
 
sign the child’s report card, ensure that the report card is returned to school, and 
include a copy of the report card in the child’s case record.
