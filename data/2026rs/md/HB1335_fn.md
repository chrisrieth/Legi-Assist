Department of Information Technology - Information Technology Staffing and 
Capability Assessment - Independent Study 
 
 
This bill requires the Department of Information Technology (DoIT) to procure the 
services of a third-party entity to conduct an independent study that includes (1) an 
assessment of information technology (IT) and cybersecurity staffing needs across the 
principal departments of the Executive Branch of State Government and 
(2) recommendations for updated IT classifications and pay scales for IT and cybersecurity 
professionals, informed by comparable compensation data from other agencies across State 
and local government. DoIT must submit the study to the Governor and General Assembly 
by November 1, 2027. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026, and terminates June 30, 2028. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  General fund expenditures increase by an estimated $250,000 in each of 
FY 2027 and 2028 for the mandated study. Revenues are not affected.

Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  The assessment of IT and cybersecurity staffing needs across principal 
departments must include:  
 
 
an inventory of existing IT and cybersecurity positions by classification, role, and 
function; 
 
an evaluation of each principal department’s IT environment, as specified; 
 
an analysis of capability gaps between current staffing levels and the skills required 
to support principal department missions; 
 
identification of the types of IT and cybersecurity roles necessary to support 
operations based on the principal department’s specific technology environment; 
and 
 
recommendations for aligning State IT staffing models with the operational, 
security, and project delivery needs of principal departments and the strategic 
priorities of DoIT’s statewide IT master plan.  
 
Current Law:  DoIT and the Secretary of Information Technology are responsible for, 
among other things:  (1) developing, maintaining, revising, and enforcing IT  
policies, procedures, and standards; (2) providing technical assistance, advice, and 
recommendations to the Governor and any unit of State government concerning IT matters; 
(3) developing, implementing, and maintaining a statewide cybersecurity strategy  
that meets specified requirements; and (4) upgrading IT and cybersecurity-related 
State government infrastructure. “IT” means all electronic information processing, 
including maintenance, telecommunications, hardware, software, and associated services. 
 
State Expenditures:  General fund expenditures increase by an estimated $250,000 in 
each of fiscal 2027 and 2028 (totaling $500,000 over the two-year period) for DoIT to 
contract with a third-party entity to conduct the required study. The estimate is based on a 
similar study undertaken by Department of Budget and Management (DBM) concerning 
compensation rates and retention for State employees more generally which, according to 
DoIT, was estimated to cost between $750,000 and $1.2 million. Because the study 
required by the bill has a narrower scope, costs are anticipated to be less than those incurred 
by DBM.  
 
For purposes of this analysis, it is assumed that costs incurred by DoIT are split equally 
between the two fiscal years that encompass the study period; however, the actual timing 
of expenditures may vary.
