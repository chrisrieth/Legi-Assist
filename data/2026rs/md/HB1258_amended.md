3 Child Placement Services – Advertising by Unlicensed Person – Prohibition
4
5 prohibiting a person from advertising adoption services in the State,
6 unless the person is a local department or a child placement agency licensed by the
7 Social Services Administration; making a violation of this Act an unfair, abusive, or
8 deceptive trade practice under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act; authorizing the
9 Attorney General to bring an action for damages for a violation of this Act; and generally
10 relating to the advertisement of adoption services by unlicensed persons.

17 5–507.

18 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, a person shall be licensed by the
19 Administration as a child placement agency before the person may engage in the placement
20 of minor children in homes or with individuals.

21 (b) A license is not required:

22 (1) for a person to place a child with an individual related to the child by
23 blood or marriage within 4 degrees of consanguinity or affinity under the civil law rule;

24 (2) except as provided in § 5–3B–12 of this title, for a parent or grandparent
25 of a child to place the child directly, without the intervention of any other person except the
26 recipient of the child; or

27 (3) for a lawyer to prepare pleadings necessary to accomplish the adoption
28 of a child or to perform any other function associated with the normal practice of law.
