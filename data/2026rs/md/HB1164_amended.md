22 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
23 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

24 Article – Public Utilities

25 1–101.

26 (a) In this division the following words have the meanings indicated.

27 (z) (1) “Public service company” means a common carrier company, electric
28 company, gas company, sewage disposal company, telegraph company, telephone company,
29 water company, or any combination of public service companies.

30 (2) “Public service company” does not include:

31 (i) a campground that provides water, electric, gas, sewage, or
32 telephone service to campers incident to the campground’s primary business of operating
33 and maintaining the campground; or

34 (ii) a person that owns or operates equipment used for charging
35 electric vehicles, including a person that owns or operates:

36 1. an electric vehicle charging station;

37 2. electric vehicle supply equipment; or

38 3. an electric vehicle charging station service company or
39 provider.

40 (gg) “Sewage disposal company” means a privately owned public service company
41 that owns or maintains facilities for the disposal of sewage.

42 (uu) “Water company” means a public service company that owns a water plant
43 and sells or distributes water for gain.

44 4–309.

45 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

46 (2) “Eligible limited–income customer” means a residential customer of a
47 utility company with annual income that:

48 (i) 1. is at or below 175% of the federal poverty level; or

49 2. for a customer at least 67 years of age, is at or below 200%
50 of the federal poverty level; or

51 (ii) meets a broader designation approved by the Commission.

52 (3) “Limited–income mechanism” or “mechanism” means a process
53 approved by the Commission under this section to benefit an eligible limited–income
54 customer of a utility company.

55 (4) “Payment plan” means an agreement between an eligible
56 limited–income customer and a utility company to pay an arrearage balance over a specific
57 period of time to avoid disconnection of a utility service.

58 (5) (i) “Utility company” means:

59 1. an electric company;

60 2. a gas and electric company;

61 3. a gas company;

62 4. A WATER COMPANY;

63 5. A WATER AND SEWAGE DISPOSAL COMPANY; OR

64 6. A SEWAGE DISPOSAL COMPANY.

65 (ii) “Utility company” does not include a small rural electric
66 cooperative.

67 (b) The General Assembly finds and declares that the societal benefits of a
68 well–constructed limited–income mechanism to benefit Maryland’s eligible limited–income
69 customers are in the public interest.

70 (c) (1) Subject to the approval of the Commission, a utility company shall
71 adopt a limited–income mechanism to benefit an eligible limited–income customer.

72 (2) Notwithstanding § 4–503(b) of this title, the mechanism may take the
73 form of a program, tariff provision, credit, rate, rider, or other means to assist an eligible
74 limited–income customer to afford a utility service.

75 (3) A municipal electric utility may adopt a limited–income mechanism
76 subject to the approval of the Commission in the same manner as a utility company in
77 accordance with this section.

78 (d) (1) A utility company that proposes a limited–income mechanism for
79 Commission approval under subsection (c) of this section shall include the proposal in:

80 (i) a separate application for approval of the mechanism; or

81 (ii) only with the prior approval of the Commission, an application
82 for a base rate proceeding, including an alternative rate proceeding, or any other proceeding
83 to alter the utility company’s base rates under the authority of the Commission.

84 (2) A proposal submitted under this section shall allocate the prudently
85 incurred costs of the limited–income mechanism across rate classes.

86 (3) The proposal shall include:

87 (i) a detailed description of the proposed mechanism;

88 (ii) the proposed method for allocating the mechanism’s costs across
89 customer classes;

90 (iii) the rationale supporting the utility company’s proposal for a
91 mechanism to benefit the eligible limited–income customers in the utility company’s service
92 territory;

93 (iv) a time frame and process for the Commission to review the
94 effectiveness of the mechanism after implementation; and

95 (v) any other information the Commission considers necessary or
96 useful to evaluate the proposal.

97 (e) In evaluating a limited–income mechanism, the Commission shall consider:

98 (1) the degree to which the mechanism promotes affordability of WATER,
99 SEWAGE DISPOSAL, electricity, or natural gas for limited–income customers;

100 (2) the public interest in allocating the costs of the mechanism between the
101 utility company’s shareholders and rate payers;

102 (3) the impact on rates, utility operating costs, customer arrearages,
103 customer disconnections, uncollectible costs, and successful completion of payment plans;

104 (4) the ability of a limited–income customer to continue to receive benefits
105 when relocating within the same service territory;

106 (5) coordination of benefits under the mechanism with any other public or
107 private assistance that may be available to the customer;

108 (6) a minimum level of support or assistance structure to provide equitable
109 availability of limited–income assistance across the State; and

110 (7) any other information the Commission considers appropriate.

111 (f) If an approved limited–income mechanism requires that the Office of Home
112 Energy Programs must certify an eligible limited–income customer’s qualifications to
113 participate in a limited–income mechanism, the Office shall certify an eligible
114 limited–income customer’s qualifications before the customer may participate in the
115 mechanism.

116 (g) An eligible limited–income customer who participates in a mechanism under
117 this section may also be eligible for other assistance programs offered in the State,
118 including those offered by a utility company or the Office of Home Energy Programs, the
119 Department of Housing and Community Development, or any other public or private
120 source.

121 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:

122 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

123 (2) “Eligible limited–income customer” and “limited–income mechanism”
124 have the meanings stated in § 4–309 of the Public Utilities Article.

125 (3) “Sewage disposal company” and “water company” have the meanings
126 stated in § 1–101 of the Public Utilities Article.

127 (b) The Public Service Commission shall study the feasibility of requiring the
128 following companies to adopt a limited–income mechanism to benefit eligible
129 limited–income customers:

130 (1) a water company;

131 (2) a water and sewage disposal company; and

132 (3) a sewage disposal company.

133 (c) On or before December 1, 2026, the Public Service Commission shall report its
134 findings to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article,
135 the General Assembly.

136 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 1 of this Act shall take
137 effect July 1, 2027.

138 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, except as provided in
139 Section 3 of this Act, this Act shall take effect July 1, 2026.
