Health Occupations - Physicians Trained in International Medical Schools 
 
 
This bill specifies that applicants for a physician license from the Maryland Board of 
Physicians (MBP) who submit evidence of successfully completing two years of training 
in a postgraduate medical education program accredited by the Accreditation Council for 
Graduate Medical Education-International (ACGME-I) are exempt from existing 
educational requirements. An individual licensed under this exemption may only practice 
medicine under supervision during their initial license term. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  There is no material impact on State revenues or expenditures. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
  
Small Business Effect:  Minimal. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Pursuant to § 14-307 of the Health Occupations Article, to qualify for a 
license as a physician, an applicant must be at least age 18, of good moral character, meet 
specified education requirements, pass an examination, submit to a criminal history records 
check, and meet any other requirements MBP establishes through regulations.  
 
An applicant must (1) have a doctoral degree of medicine that is accredited by an  
MBP-recognized organization and submit evidence to MBP of successful completion of 
one year of training in a postgraduate medical training program that is accredited by an 
MBP-recognized organization or (2) have a doctoral degree of osteopathy from a school of 
osteopathy in the United States, its territories or possessions, Puerto Rico, or Canada that 
has standards for graduation equivalent to those established by the American Osteopathic

Association and submit acceptable evidence to MBP of successful completion of one year 
of training in a postgraduate medical training program that is accredited by an

However, under current law, an applicant for a physician license is exempt from the above 
specified educational requirements if the applicant:   
 
 
has studied medicine at a foreign medical school; 
 
is certified by the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates or its 
successor as approved by MBP; 
 
passes a qualifying examination for foreign medical school graduates required by 
MBP;  
 
meets any other qualifications for foreign medical school graduates that MBP 
establishes in its regulation for licensing of applicants;  
 
submits acceptable evidence to MBP of any requirements established in regulation; 
and  
 
meets at least one of the following requirements:  (1) the applicant graduated from 
any foreign medical school and submits acceptable evidence to MBP of successful 
completion of two years of training in a postgraduate medical education program 
accredited by an MBP-recognized organization or (2) the applicant successfully 
completed a fifth pathway program and submits specified evidence to MBP.  
 
Additional Comments:  Guidance from the Advisory Commission on Additional 
Licensing Models included ACGME-I as a possible resource for determining whether an 
international program is substantially similar to existing educational requirements, but did 
not explicitly recommend states adopt it.
