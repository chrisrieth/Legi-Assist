Code Revision - Higher Education - Janet L. Hoffman Loan Assistance 
Repayment Program 
 
 
This bill primarily revises, restates, and recodifies the laws of the State related to the  
Janet L. Hoffman Loan Assistance Repayment Program (LARP) through a series of 
clarifications and technical and stylistic changes. However, the bill alters eligibility for 
lawyer, licensed clinical counselor, and teacher applicants to the Janet L. Hoffman LARP. 
Specifically, the bill (1) limits eligibility for lawyers to graduates of law schools located in 
the State, rather than graduates of any law school; (2) removes higher-education degree 
requirements for licensed clinical alcohol and drug counselors, licensed clinical marriage 
and family therapy counselors, and licensed clinical professional counselors; and  
(3) narrows eligibility for teachers by requiring all teacher applicants to qualify for the 
Nancy Grasmick Public School Professional Award. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  Altering eligibility for the Janet L. Hoffman LARP does not affect program 
spending, though Maryland Higher Education Commission (MHEC) general fund 
expenditures likely minimally increase for one-time programming costs in FY 2027. 
Otherwise, the bill’s provisions are nonsubstantive revisions, restatements, and 
recodifications resulting in no fiscal impact. Revenues are not affected. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None.

Bill Summary/Current Law: 
 
Janet L. Hoffman Loan Assistance Repayment Program 
 
The Janet L. Hoffman LARP is administered by the Office of Student Financial Assistance 
within MHEC to assist in the repayment of any higher education loan owed by an 
individual who: 
 
 
either (1) receives a graduate, professional, or undergraduate degree from a college 
or university in the State, a law school, or, for mental health professionals only, any 
accredited college or university or (2) receives a Resident Teacher Certificate from 
the Maryland State Department of Education after completing an approved 
alternative teaching preparation program; 
 
obtains eligible employment; 
 
receives an income less than the maximum eligible total income levels established 
by the office, including any additional sources of income; and 
 
satisfies any other criteria established. 
 
Eligible employment fields include lawyers, registered nurses, physical and occupational 
therapists, social workers, speech pathologists, teachers, and certain school health 
professionals. 
 
Specified licensed clinical counselors who practice in a high-need geographic area may 
also qualify. Chapters 387 and 388 of 2025 expanded the program to include veterinary 
practitioners and veterinary technicians who hold degrees from accredited veterinary 
programs and provide specified services. 
 
Awards are based on reported educational debt and distributed annually over a 
three-year period, provided recipients maintain eligibility and submit required 
documentation each year. 
 
Nancy Grasmick Teacher Award 
 
Chapter 496 of 2012 established the Nancy Grasmick Teacher Award within  
Janet L. Hoffman LARP for Maryland. Chapter 627 of 2023 expanded eligibility to 
specified mental health professionals (renaming the program the Nancy Grasmick Public 
School Professional Award), and Chapters 250 and 251 of 2024 added public school 
nurses. The award is available to public school teachers, mental health professionals, and 
nurses who have worked in Maryland public schools for at least two years and who meet

Teacher Eligibility Outside the Nancy Grasmick Public School Professional Award 
 
A teacher may receive loan repayment assistance through the Janet L. Hoffman LARP by 
meeting the program’s general eligibility requirements. Specifically, a teacher must be 
employed in eligible public service, meet applicable income limits, and hold a qualifying 
degree from a Maryland institution or a resident teacher certificate obtained through an 
approved alternative teaching preparation program. These requirements apply regardless 
of whether the teacher qualifies for a Nancy Grasmick Public School Professional Award. 
 
By contrast, eligibility for a Nancy Grasmick Public School Professional Award requires a 
teacher, in addition to meeting the general Hoffman eligibility requirements, to have taught 
in a public school for at least two years in specified subject areas or high-poverty schools 
and to have received the highest available performance evaluation rating, as set forth in 
regulation. 
 
Educational Requirements for Licensed Counselors 
 
Licensed clinical alcohol and drug counselors, licensed clinical marriage and family 
therapists, and licensed clinical professional counselors must hold at least a master’s degree 
(or higher) in a relevant counseling or therapy field from an accredited educational 
institution, with additional coursework and training requirements established by the  
State Board of Professional Counselors and Therapists. 
 
Code Revision Provisions 
 
Otherwise, the bill repeals, recodifies, and reorganizes Janet L. Hoffman LARP provisions, 
including correcting cross-references and making stylistic changes. The bill generally 
re-orders provisions relating to the Janet L. Hoffman LARP to more clearly delineate 
eligibility provisions for each of the LARP’s categories of eligibility. 
 
The bill authorizes the publisher of the Annotated Code of Maryland, in consultation with 
and subject to the approval of the Department of Legislative Services (DLS), to correct 
cross-references and terminology rendered incorrect by the bill or by any other Act of the 
General Assembly of 2026 that affects provisions enacted by the bill with no further action 
required by the General Assembly. The publisher must adequately describe any correction 
in an editor’s note following the section affected.

State Expenditures:  Overall, the bill does not result in a material change in general fund 
expenditures. Although the bill alters eligibility criteria for the Janet L. Hoffman LARP, 
these changes do not necessarily affect spending, as detailed below. 
 
Altering eligibility does not necessarily require additional spending. To the extent the bill 
expands eligibility for the Janet L. Hoffman LARP in the aggregate – by expanding 
eligibility for licensed clinical counselors – and to the extent existing appropriated funds 
are insufficient to cover additional awards, an estimated $5,300 per award is necessary to 
make additional awards based on fiscal 2025 awards data for the program. Otherwise, 
individuals can be added to the Janet L. Hoffman LARP waiting list with no additional 
resources. Any such impact is likely immaterial due to licensed clinical counselors 
requiring at least a master’s degree to be licensed in the State. 
 
Conversely, to the extent the bill narrows eligibility in the aggregate – by narrowing 
eligibility for lawyers to just those who attended a law school in the State and teachers to 
only include teachers who meet the Nancy Grasmick Public School Professional Award 
requirements – overall spending likely remains unaffected as MHEC may instead make 
additional awards to applicants from other professions. 
 
The fiscal 2027 budget as introduced includes $4.1 million in funding for the program. The 
program also received $4.1 million in fiscal 2026. Originally, $7.1 million was allocated 
for the program in fiscal 2025; however, the fiscal 2026 budget includes a $3.0 million 
reduction to the fiscal 2025 appropriation. In fiscal 2024, the program received 
$6.4 million. 
 
There may be additional indeterminate, but likely minimal, one-time costs associated with 
enhancing MHEC’s existing Maryland College Aid Processing System (better known as 
MDCAPS) to reflect changes in eligibility in fiscal 2027. 
 
Additional Comments:  While not part of the DLS formal code revision project, which 
was completed in 2016 with the reenactment of the Alcoholic Beverages Article, this 
revision was initiated by DLS under its statutory responsibility to maintain the clarity, 
simplicity, and consistency of style of statutory law. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.
