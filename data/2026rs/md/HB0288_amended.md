1 (c) “Prolonged state of emergency” means a state of emergency declared by the
2 [Governor under Title 14 of the Public Safety Article] STATE SUPERINTENDENT UNDER § 7–14A–02 OF THIS SUBTITLE that
3 prevents regular, in–person attendance at a public school for at least 14 consecutive school
4 days.

5 (e) “Virtual education” means academic instruction:

6 (1) In which one or more technologies are used to deliver a significant
7 portion of the teaching to students entirely or primarily online; and

8 (2) In which students and instructors participate remotely from separate
9 locations.

10 7–14A–02.

11 (A) ON WRITTEN REQUEST OF A COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT OR A
12 DETERMINATION BY THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT THAT IMMEDIATE ACTION IS
13 NECESSARY, THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT MAY DECLARE A PROLONGED STATE
14 OF EMERGENCY THAT PREVENTS REGULAR, IN–PERSON ATTENDANCE AT A
15 PUBLIC SCHOOL FOR AT LEAST 14 CONSECUTIVE SCHOOL DAYS DUE TO:

16 (1) A NATURAL DISASTER;

17 (2) A CIVIL DISASTER;

18 (3) A PUBLIC HEALTH EMERGENCY; OR

19 (4) A SPECIFIC SECURITY THREAT IDENTIFIED BY FEDERAL,
20 STATE, OR LOCAL LAW ENFORCEMENT.

21 (B) IN MAKING A DECLARATION OF A PROLONGED STATE OF EMERGENCY
22 UNDER THIS SECTION, THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT SHALL:

23 (1) CONSULT THE GOVERNOR, AND IF APPROPRIATE:

24 (I) THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF EMERGENCY
25 MANAGEMENT;

26 (II) THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE POLICE; OR

27 (III) THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH;

28 (2) ISSUE THE DECLARATION IN WRITING;

29 (3) INDICATE IN THE DECLARATION:

30 (I) THE NATURE OF THE EMERGENCY; AND

31 (II) THE SPECIFIC LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS OR INDIVIDUAL
32 SCHOOLS THREATENED OR AFFECTED, UNLESS THE EMERGENCY IS STATEWIDE
33 IN NATURE; AND

34 (4) DISSEMINATE THE DECLARATION PROMPTLY BY MEANS
35 CALCULATED TO PUBLICIZE THE CONTENTS OF THE DECLARATION.

36 (C) IF AN EMERGENCY THREATENS OR AFFECTS A PARTICULAR SCHOOL
37 WITHIN A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM, THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT MAY DECLARE
38 A PROLONGED STATE OF EMERGENCY FOR THAT SCHOOL.

39 (D) (1) A PROLONGED STATE OF EMERGENCY DECLARED IN
40 ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION MAY BE TERMINATED BY:

41 (I) A JOINT RESOLUTION BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY;

42 (II) AN AFFIRMATIVE VOTE BY THE STATE BOARD; OR

43 (III) A DECISION BY THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT.

44 (2) IF A PROLONGED STATE OF EMERGENCY IS TERMINATED BY
45 ANY METHOD LISTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE STATE
46 SUPERINTENDENT SHALL ISSUE A DECLARATION DISSEMINATED PROMPTLY BY
47 MEANS CALCULATED TO PUBLICIZE THE TERMINATION OF THE PROLONGED
48 STATE OF EMERGENCY.

49 (E) NOTHING IN THIS SUBTITLE MAY BE CONSTRUED TO LIMIT OR
50 INTERFERE WITH THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE SECRETARY OF HEALTH
51 UNDER TITLE 18 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE IN THE EVENT OF A
52 PUBLIC HEALTH EMERGENCY.

53 7–14A–03.

11 (a) If there is a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM, a county board may transition ONE OR MORE
12 public schools in the county under an approved virtual education plan,
13 subject to the limitations of this section.

14 (b) (1) (i) On or before June 1, 2024, a county board shall, in consultation
15 with county administrators and school staff, adopt a virtual education plan for use during
16 a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.

17 (ii) A county board shall use the county board’s continuity of learning
18 plan in effect during the 2020–2021 school year to form a virtual education plan.

19 (2) A virtual education plan shall include the following components:

20 (i) A staffing and personnel assignment plan;

21 (ii) A student instruction plan;

22 (iii) A student assessment and learning support plan;

23 (iv) A social and emotional learning plan;

24 (v) A community communications plan;

25 (vi) A technology plan;

26 (vii) A nutritional and health services plan; and

27 (viii) A plan for return to in–person instruction.

28 (c) A county board shall update the virtual education plan every 2 years.

29 (d) (1) If a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM requires the cessation of in–school
