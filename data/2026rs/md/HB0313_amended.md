A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Landlord and Tenant – Residential Housing – Rental Applications and Tenant
3 Screening

4 FOR the purpose of requiring a landlord to
5 provide certain notice and documentation to a prospective tenant under certain
6 circumstances; providing that a prospective tenant shall be allowed to dispute and
7 provide evidence regarding certain information contained in a tenant screening
8 report; prohibiting a landlord from taking certain actions relating to a certain court
9 proceeding and record; ESTABLISHING THAT A LANDLORD IS NOT LIABLE FOR
10 CERTAIN INFORMATION INCLUDED IN A TENANT SCREENING REPORT UNDER
11 CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES AND REQUIRING A LANDLORD TO REPORT CERTAIN
12 INFORMATION TO A PERSON THAT PRODUCES TENANT SCREENING REPORTS;
13 establishing certain requirements for a person that produces
14 tenant screening reports; and generally relating to rental applications
15 and prospective tenant screening.

17 Article – Real Property
18 Section 8–222
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

24 (I) THE LANDLORD RELIED IN GOOD FAITH ON A TENANT

26 (II) THE LANDLORD DID NOT HAVE ACTUAL KNOWLEDGE
27 THAT THE RECORD WAS SHIELDED.

28 (E) (1) A PERSON THAT PRODUCES TENANT SCREENING REPORTS:

29 (I) SHALL IMPLEMENT AND MAINTAIN REASONABLE
30 PROCEDURES TO PREVENT THE DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION REGARDING A
31 SHIELDED RECORD;

32 (II) MAY NOT DISCLOSE INFORMATION ABOUT A SHIELDED
33 RECORD; AND

34 (III) FOR AN UNSHIELDED RECORD, SHALL CLEARLY AND
35 ACCURATELY INDICATE THE DISPOSITION OR OUTCOME OF THE CLAIM AGAINST
36 THE SUBJECT OF THE TENANT SCREENING REPORT.

37 (2) A PERSON THAT DISCLOSES INFORMATION OF A SHIELDED RECORD
38 SHALL PROVIDE TO ALL PARTIES A CORRECTED TENANT SCREENING REPORT WITH
39 THE IMPROPERLY DISCLOSED INFORMATION REMOVED WITHIN 5 BUSINESS DAYS
40 AFTER LEARNING OF THE DISCLOSURE.

41 (F) A PROSPECTIVE TENANT MAY NOT WAIVE ANY OF THE PROTECTIONS
