Oversight Committee on Quality of Care in Nursing Homes and Assisted Living 
Facilities – Membership 
 
 
This bill alters the membership of the Oversight Committee on Quality of Care in Nursing 
Homes and Assisted Living Facilities by (1) removing one member of the  
Senate Education, Health, and Environmental Affairs Committee (now known as the 
Senate Education, Energy, and the Environment Committee); (2) reducing from two to 
one the number of members from the House Health Committee; (3) removing  
one representative from the Health Facilities Association of Maryland; (4) removing  
one representative from Voices for Quality Care; and (5) adding one representative from 
LeadingAge Maryland. The bill requires the three representatives from the assisted living 
industry to represent specified programs to the extent practicable and makes a technical 
change to correct the name of the Maryland Association for Medical Adult Day Services. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The change is technical in nature and does not directly affect 
governmental finances. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  In general, the oversight committee evaluates progress in improving the 
quality of nursing homes and assisted living facilities in the State, including consideration 
of (1) standards for the identification of specified diseases and conditions; (2) staffing

The oversight committee consists of one member of the Senate Finance Committee; 
one member of the Senate Education, Health, and Environmental Affairs Committee; 
two members of the House Health and Government Operations Committee (now known as 
the House Health Committee); the Secretaries of Aging, Health, Human Services, and 
Disabilities (or their designee); the Director of the Office of Health Care Quality (or their 
designee); the Deputy Secretary for Behavioral Health (or their designee); the  
State Long-Term Care Ombudsman; two representatives of areas agencies on aging; 
one representative of a local long-term care ombudsman entity; three consumer members; 
three representatives from the assisted living industry, of which one must represent a 
program that cares for 1 to 4 residents, one must represent a program that cares for 5 to  
9 residents, and one must represent a program that cares for more than 10 residents; and 
several representatives from specified organizations, including:



Additional Comments:  The Senate Education, Energy, and the Environment Committee 
(commonly known as EEE) was reorganized and renamed in December 2022 from the 
Senate Education, Health, and Environmental Affairs Committee. As part of this 
reorganization, all health matters previously overseen by EEE were transferred to the 
Senate Finance Committee.
