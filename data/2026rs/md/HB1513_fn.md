Calvert County - Public Facilities Bonds 
 
 
This bill authorizes the Calvert County Commissioners to issue up to $81.0 million in 
general obligation (GO) bonds for the acquisition, construction, improvement, or 
renovation of public buildings, facilities, and public works projects. The date of maturity 
of the bonds cannot exceed 30 years. The bill takes effect June 1, 2026.  
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. 
  
Local Effect:  Calvert County receives up to $81.0 million in bond proceeds. County debt 
service expenditures increase by an estimated $6.3 million annually. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Commission counties do not have the legislative power to create debt; 
General Assembly authorization is required before any bonds can be sold. Calvert County 
is one of six counties that still operates under the commission form of government. 
 
Local Fiscal Effect:  Since 2015, Calvert County has received authorization by the 
General Assembly to issue up to $240.5 million in GO bonds for public facilities, as shown 
in Exhibit 1. Exhibit 2 shows the planned capital projects in Calvert County for 
fiscal 2027.

Calvert County revenues increase by up to $81.0 million from bond proceeds. Annual debt 
service costs total approximately $4.6 million for bonds issued with a 15-year term of 
maturity and $1.7 million for bonds issued with a 25-year term of maturity. This estimate 
is based on a 3.23% interest rate and 4.15% interest rate, respectively. To the extent that 
the bond issuance, interest rates, or terms of maturity deviate from this assumption, debt 
service expenditures would adjust accordingly. 
 
At the end of fiscal 2024, Calvert County had approximately $193.6 million in total 
outstanding debt, which represents approximately 1.0% of the county’s assessable base and 
$2,042 on a per capita basis. The county currently has an AAA credit rating from 
Standard and Poor’s, an Aaa credit rating from Moody’s Investors Service, and an 
AAA credit rating from Fitch Ratings. 
 
Information on local debt measures for Maryland counties and Baltimore City can be found 
in the Local Government Overview report. A copy of the 2026 report is available on the 
Department of Legislative Services website.
