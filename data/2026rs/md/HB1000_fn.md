Public Schools - School Mapping Data - Radius of School Grounds 
 
 
This bill requires that, beginning July 1, 2026, school mapping data produced with funding 
from the School Mapping Data Program contain site-specific labeling for bodies of water 
in the one-mile radius surrounding the school grounds. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  The bill likely has no material effect on general fund expenditures as the 
required labeling can be incorporated into future maps with existing resources. However, 
to the extent that existing maps must be updated with the required labeling, general fund 
expenditures may increase, as discussed below. The Interagency Commission on School 
Construction (IAC) can update mapping standards with existing resources. No effect on 
revenues.  
  
Local Effect:  Local school system can likely incorporate the required labelling into  
future school maps with existing grant funding, but if the bill is interpreted to require 
updating existing maps, local revenues and expenditures may increase, potentially 
significantly, as discussed below. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Chapters 166 and 167 of 2024 established the School Mapping Data 
Program in the Maryland Center for School Safety (MCSS) to provide funds to local school 
systems to produce school mapping data for each public school in the county, including 
charter schools. Local school systems can apply for funding from the program, and any

school mapping data produced with funding from the program must comply with standards 
developed by IAC. The Acts further specified minimum requirements for school mapping 
data, including that it be (1) compatible with software platforms used by the school and 
public safety agencies that provide emergency services to the school, as specified and  
(2) be verified for accuracy by a walk-through of the school buildings and grounds. 
 
The fiscal 2026 budget, as enacted, included an appropriation of $3.0 million for the 
program. 
 
State/Local Fiscal Effect:  MCSS advises that, of the original $3.0 million appropriation 
for the School Mapping Data Program, it has awarded $2.3 million to local school systems, 
covering 976 schools. This leaves 446 schools without grant funding and $700,000 in 
unallocated funds. 
 
IAC, which is responsible for developing mapping standards and has worked with the 
Department of Information Technology to make data available for the maps, advises that 
schools that have not yet developed their maps with the grant funding can incorporate the 
required labeling of bodies of water with existing grant funds. However, IAC notes that the 
bill is not clear regarding the status of school maps that have already been created. The bill 
specifies that maps must include the labeling for bodies of water beginning July 1, 2026, 
but does not specify whether this requirement applies only to maps created after that date, 
or whether existing maps must also add the labeling by that date. 
 
IAC advises that adding labeling to existing maps is costly and likely requires additional 
grant funding for each map, although a reliable estimate is not feasible. Thus, to the extent 
that the intent of the bill is to require only new maps created on or after July 1, 2026, to 
incorporate the labeling, general fund expenditures are not affected. However, to the extent 
that the bill applies to existing maps, general fund expenditures increase by an 
indeterminate but potentially significant amount to provide additional grant funding to 
local school systems, and local school system revenues and expenditures increase 
commensurately to add the labeling to existing maps. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years. 
 
Designated Cross File:  None.
