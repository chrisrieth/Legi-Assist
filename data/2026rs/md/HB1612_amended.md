Assigned to: Rules and Executive Nominations

A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Gaming – Campaign Financing Contributions and Study on Independent Evaluation of
3 Sports Wagering

4 FOR the purpose of repealing the prohibition on applicants for and holders of video lottery
5 operation licenses and persons who own an interest in video lottery facility
6 operations in the State from directly or indirectly making contributions to certain
7 campaign finance entities; requiring the State Lottery and Gaming Control Agency
8 to study and report to the Governor and the General Assembly, on or before a certain
9 date, on certain matters concerning independent evaluators for sports wagering
10 content; and generally relating to gaming.

11 BY repealing
12 Article – Election Law
13 Section 13–237
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

18 Article – Election Law

19 [13–237.

20 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

21 (2) “Own” has the meaning stated in § 9–1A–01 of the State
22 Government Article.

23 (3) “Video lottery facility” has the meaning stated in § 9–1A–01 of the
24 State Government Article.

25 (4) “Video lottery operation license” has the meaning stated in § 9–1A–
26 01 of the State Government Article.

27 (b) This section applies to the following persons:

28 (1) an applicant for a video lottery operation license;

29 (2) a holder of a video lottery operation license; or

30 (3) a person who owns an interest in the operation of a video lottery
31 facility in this State.

32 (c) This section does not apply to gaming activity that an eligible organization
33 is authorized to conduct under the Criminal Law Article.

34 (d) A person subject to this section may not, directly or indirectly, make a
35 contribution to:

36 (1) the campaign finance entity of a candidate for any nonfederal public
37 office in the State; or

38 (2) any other campaign finance entity organized in support of a
39 candidate for any nonfederal public office in the State.]

40 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before January 1,
41 2027, the State Lottery and Gaming Control Agency shall study and report to the
42 Governor and the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
43 Government Article, on:

44 (1) the number of independent evaluators licensed by the State Lottery
45 and Gaming Control Commission under § 9–1E–17 of the State Government Article;

46 (2) the utilization of independent evaluators by sports wagering
47 licensees and sports wagering operators in the State to evaluate sports wagering content
48 in accordance with § 9–1E–17 of the State Government Article;

49 (3) the utilization of independent evaluators by sports wagering
50 licensees and sports wagering operators in other states to evaluate sports wagering
51 content; and

52 (4) whether any other states require sports wagering licensees or sports
53 wagering operators licensed in those states to contract with independent evaluators to
54 evaluate sports wagering content.

55 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 1 of this Act shall
56 take effect January 1, 2027.
