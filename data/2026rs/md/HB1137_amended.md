A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Residential–in–Commercial–Zone Laws – Study

4 (Bring Back Main Street Act)

5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Housing and Community
6 Development to study and make recommendations regarding laws to require
7 certain local jurisdictions to allow certain residential or mixed–use
8 developments as a permitted use on certain parcels or lots zoned for
9 commercial use; and generally relating to residential–in–commercial–zone
10 laws.

6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That:

8 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

9 (2) “Department” means the Department of Housing and Community
10 Development.

11 (3) “Residential–in–commercial–zone (RICZ) law” means statewide
12 legislation that requires local jurisdictions to allow multifamily residential or mixed–
13 use developments as a permitted use in at least some commercially zoned areas.

14 (b) The Department shall conduct a study of RICZ laws, which shall include:

15 (1) a summary and comparison of RICZ laws enacted by other states;

16 (2) an assessment of the impact of RICZ laws on:

17 (i) urban communities where there is already a mix of
18 commercial and residential uses;

19 (ii) local infrastructure;

20 (iii) local economic development goals; and

21 (iv) housing production; and

22 (3) recommendations regarding appropriate requirements and
23 restrictions applicable to multifamily residential and mixed–use development under
24 RICZ laws, including recommendations regarding:

25 (i) off–street parking requirements;

26 (ii) allowed density;

27 (iii) first–floor nonresidential use requirements;

28 (iv) building height restrictions;

29 (v) the types of commercial areas appropriate for multifamily
30 residential and mixed–use developments; and

31 (vi) special considerations for urban, suburban, and rural areas.
