CHAPTER ______

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Juvenile Law – Confinement and Restrictive Housing – Limitations

3 altering certain requirements for regulations adopted by the Department of Juvenile
4 Services relating to the placement of a minor in restrictive housing and
5 confinement;

6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Human Services
8 Section 9–227(b)(2)
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

13 Article – Human Services

14 9–227.

15 (b) The Department shall:

16 (2) adopt regulations applicable to residential facilities it operates that:

17 (i) prohibit the use of locked door seclusion and restraints as
18 punishment [and describe the circumstances under which locked door seclusion and
19 restraints may be used], FOR CONVENIENCE, AS A STAFFING SUBSTITUTE, OR IN
20 RETALIATION;

21 (II) SPECIFY THAT LOCKED DOOR SECLUSION AND RESTRAINTS
22 MAY BE USED ONLY WHEN ALL LESS RESTRICTIVE MEASURES HAVE BEEN
23 EXHAUSTED;

24 (III) LIMIT THE USE OF LOCKED DOOR SECLUSION AND
25 RESTRAINTS TO THOSE INSTANCES WHERE THERE IS AN IMMEDIATE AND
26 SUBSTANTIAL RISK OF PHYSICAL HARM TO THE CHILD OR OTHERS, OR IMMINENT
27 RISK OF ESCAPE;

28 (IV) REQUIRE THAT THE USE OF ANY LOCKED DOOR SECLUSION:

29 1. BE TRAUMA–INFORMED, DEVELOPMENTALLY
30 APPROPRIATE, AND TIME–LIMITED;

31 2. REQUIRE VISUAL INSPECTION OF THE CHILD BY
32 DIRECT CARE STAFF IN INTERVALS OF LESS THAN 15 MINUTES;

33 3. REQUIRE AN INITIAL MEDICAL AND BEHAVIORAL
34 HEALTH ASSESSMENT OF THE CHILD TO BE CONDUCTED NOT LATER THAN 30
35 MINUTES AFTER THE INITIATION OF THE LOCKED DOOR SECLUSION;

36 4. REQUIRE REPEATED MEDICAL AND BEHAVIORAL
37 HEALTH ASSESSMENTS AT REGULAR INTERVALS FOLLOWING AN INITIAL MEDICAL
38 EXAMINATION UNDER ITEM 3 OF THIS ITEM;

39 5. PROVIDE SCHEDULED MEALS AND SNACKS, ACCESS
40 TO WATER, BATHROOM ACCESS, ACCESS TO EDUCATIONAL SERVICES AND
41 MATERIALS, AND ACCESS TO HYGIENE ITEMS;

42 6. BE TERMINATED IMMEDIATELY ONCE THE
43 IMMEDIATE AND SUBSTANTIAL THREAT HAS PASSED; AND

44 7. BE FOLLOWED BY A DEBRIEFING WITH A BEHAVIORAL
45 HEALTH REVIEW TO PREVENT FUTURE USE FOR THE CHILD; and

46 [(ii)] (V) prohibit abuse of a child; and

47 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
48 October 1, 2026.
