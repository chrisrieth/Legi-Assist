26 (1) Wearing apparel, books, tools, instruments, or appliances, in an amount
27 not to exceed $5,000 in value necessary for the practice of any trade or profession except

30 the event of sickness, accident, injury, or death of any person, including compensation for

32 account of judgments, arbitrations, compromises, insurance, benefits, compensation, and
33 relief. Disability income benefits are not exempt if the judgment is for necessities contracted
34 for after the disability is incurred.

35 (3) Professionally prescribed health aids for the debtor or any dependent of
36 the debtor.

37 (4) (I) [The] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
38 PARAGRAPH, THE debtor’s interest, not to exceed [$1,000] $5,000 in value, in household
39 furnishings, household goods, wearing apparel, appliances, books, animals kept as pets,
40 and other items that are held primarily for the personal, family, or household use of the
41 debtor or any dependent of the debtor.

42 (II) FOR A DEBTOR WHO IS ELDERLY OR WHO HAS A DISABILITY,
43 THE DEBTOR’S INTEREST, NOT TO EXCEED $10,000 IN VALUE, IN HOUSEHOLD
44 FURNISHINGS, HOUSEHOLD GOODS, WEARING APPAREL, APPLIANCES, BOOKS,
45 ANIMALS KEPT AS PETS, AND OTHER ITEMS THAT ARE HELD PRIMARILY FOR THE
46 PERSONAL, FAMILY, OR HOUSEHOLD USE OF THE DEBTOR OR ANY DEPENDENT OF
47 THE DEBTOR.
