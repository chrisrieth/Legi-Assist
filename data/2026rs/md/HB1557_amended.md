2 Procurement – Real Estate Advisory
3 Committee and Property Acquisition and Lease Requirements

4 FOR the purpose of establishing a Real Estate Advisory Committee in the Department of
5 General Services to advise the Department, the Board of Public Works, and the
6 General Assembly on certain real estate matters; establishing additional
7 requirements for the Board of Public Works before approving the acquisition or lease
8 of certain real property; and generally

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

3 Article – State Finance and Procurement

4 4–417.

5 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “COMMITTEE” MEANS THE REAL ESTATE
6 COMMITTEE.

7 (B) THERE IS A REAL ESTATE COMMITTEE IN THE
8 DEPARTMENT.

9 (C) THE PURPOSE OF THE COMMITTEE IS TO ADVISE THE DEPARTMENT,
10 THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS, AND THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON INDUSTRY
11 TRENDS, DEVELOPMENTS AND MOVEMENT IN THE COMMERCIAL REAL ESTATE
12 SECTOR, AND BEST PRACTICES FOR REAL ESTATE ASSET MANAGEMENT IN THE
13 STATE.

14 (D) THE COMMITTEE CONSISTS OF THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:

15 (1) THE SECRETARY, OR THE SECRETARY’S DESIGNEE;

16 (2) FOUR MEMBERS APPOINTED BY THE SECRETARY;

17 (3) ONE MEMBER APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE;
18 AND

19 (4) ONE MEMBER APPOINTED BY THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE.

20 (E) THE SECRETARY SHALL DESIGNATE THE CHAIR OF THE
21 COMMITTEE.

22 (F) THE DEPARTMENT AND THE DEPARTMENT OF LEGISLATIVE
23 SERVICES SHALL PROVIDE STAFF FOR THE COMMITTEE.

24 (G) A MEMBER OF THE COMMITTEE:

25 (1) MAY NOT RECEIVE COMPENSATION AS A MEMBER OF THE
26 COMMITTEE; BUT

27 (2) IS ENTITLED TO REIMBURSEMENT FOR EXPENSES UNDER THE
28 STANDARD STATE TRAVEL REGULATIONS, AS PROVIDED IN THE STATE BUDGET.

29 (H) THE COMMITTEE:

30 (1) SHALL MEET AT LEAST THREE TIMES ANNUALLY; AND

31 (2) SHALL REVIEW, AT LEAST ONCE EACH YEAR, THE STATE’S
32 LONG–TERM REAL ESTATE PLANS AND GOALS.

33 (I) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2026, THE
34 COMMITTEE SHALL SUBMIT ITS FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE
35 GOVERNOR, THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS, THE DEPARTMENT, AND, IN
36 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL
37 ASSEMBLY.
