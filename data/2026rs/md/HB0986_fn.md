Nurse Licensure - Educational Requirements - Enforcement 
 
 
This bill requires an applicant for licensure by endorsement from the State Board of 
Nursing (MBON) to provide adequate evidence, including official transcripts, that the 
applicantŌĆÖs educational credentials meet specified requirements. The bill expands the 
disciplinary grounds on which MBON may take disciplinary action against an applicant or 
licensee to include failure to complete specified educational requirements. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The change is technical in nature and does not directly affect 
governmental finances. 
 
Local Effect:  None. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Generally, an individual must be licensed and/or certified by MBON to 
practice specified nursing duties in the State. MBON is responsible for the regulation and 
oversight of nursing professionals in the State, ensuring that nurses comply with federal 
and State laws governing the profession through the regulation and oversight of education 
and training programs and by taking disciplinary action against licensees and certificate 
holders when warranted.

Licensure by Endorsement 
 
MBON may issue a license by endorsement and waive any appropriate examination 
requirement for an applicant who has an active unencumbered registered nurse (RN) or 
licensed practical nurse (LPN) license in any other state or country if the applicant:

’éĘ 
submits to a criminal history records check;

program approved in Maryland; (2) passed an examination at the time they were 
licensed that is similar to the Maryland examination; and (3) meet the qualifications 
otherwise required. 
 
Chapters 731 and 732 of 2025 clarified that an applicant may hold a valid, unencumbered 
multistate license under the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) as an alternative to providing 
the evidence specified above to qualify for licensure by endorsement.  
 
Chapter 416 of 2025 specified that, if an applicant for licensure as an RN by endorsement 
cannot provide evidence that the nursing program they graduated from was substantially 
equivalent to the education program approved in Maryland, the applicant may still qualify 
for licensure by endorsement if the applicant has:  
 
’éĘ

MBON or an applicable state regulator in the state in which the program is located; 
and  
’éĘ 
completed a minimum number of direct patient care practice hours as required by 
regulations adopted by MBON.  
 
Disciplinary Actions 
 
Subject to specified hearing provisions, MBON may deny a license or grant a license, 
including a license subject to reprimand, probation, or suspension, to an applicant, 
reprimand a licensee, place a licensee on probation, or suspend or revoke the license of a 
licensee if, among other things, the applicant or licensee violates any one of 36 disciplinary 
grounds.

Nurse Licensure Compact  
 
Since 1999, Maryland has participated in NLC, which currently has 43 participating states 
and territories. Under NLC, a multistate license authorizes an individual to practice as an 
RN or an LPN in a remote state. Licenses are issued by the home state.
