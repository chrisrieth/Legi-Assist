1 Article – Family Law

2 4–505.

3 (a) (1) (i) If, after a hearing on a petition, whether ex parte or otherwise, a
4 judge finds that there are reasonable grounds to believe that a person eligible for relief has
5 been abused, the judge may enter a temporary protective order to protect any person
6 eligible for relief from abuse.

7 (c) (1) Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, the temporary
8 protective order shall be effective for not more than [7] 14 days after service of the order.

9 (2) The judge may extend the temporary protective order as needed, but
10 not to exceed 6 months, to effectuate service of the order where necessary to provide
11 protection or for other good cause.

12 (3) If the court is closed on the day on which the temporary protective order
13 is due to expire, the temporary protective order shall be effective until the second day on
14 which the court is open, by which time the court shall hold a final protective order hearing.

15 4–506.

16 (a) A respondent under § 4–505 of this subtitle shall have an opportunity to be
17 heard on the question of whether the judge should issue a final protective order.

18 (b) (1) (i) The temporary protective order shall state the date and time of
19 the final protective order hearing.

20 (ii) Except as provided in § 4–505(c) of this subtitle, or unless
21 continued for good cause, the final protective order hearing shall be held no later than [7]
22 14 days after the temporary protective order is served on the respondent.
