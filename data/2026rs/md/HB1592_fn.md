Education - Regional Resource Centers and County Libraries - Funding 
 
 
This bill increases the per capita funding amount that must be provided to each regional 
resource center and to each county public library system participating in the State’s library 
program beginning in fiscal 2028. The bill also alters the types of services that each public 
library must offer. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  General fund expenditures increase by $1.9 million in FY 2028 and by 
$8.0 million in FY 2031. Future year expenditures reflect per capita aid adjustments and 
population increases. Revenues are not affected. This bill increases a mandated 
appropriation beginning in FY 2028.

Required Services  
 
The bill alters current statutory provisions that require libraries to offer certain services. 
Specifically, each public library must offer at least one of the following services (alone or 
in partnership with units of government or nonprofit organizations):   
 
 
a program designed to increase early childhood literacy and learning, school 
readiness, literacy proficiency, and student success; 
 
an educational program that advances information literacy, technology instruction 
and skills, and economic, career, and workforce development; 
 
an initiative that increases digital equity and access to the Internet;  
 
serving as a community resource and distribution site during public emergencies 
(required under current law); and 
 
support for mental health and personal well-being.

Current Law:  
 
State Library Network  
 
The State provides funds to libraries designated as resource centers, including the 
State Library Resource Center in Baltimore City and to regional resource centers, including 
the Eastern Resource Center in Salisbury, the Southern Resource Center in Charlotte Hall, 
and the Western Resource Center in Hagerstown. 
 
Minimum Per capita Library Program (Library Aid Formula) 
 
The State provides assistance to public libraries through a formula that determines the 
State and local shares of a minimum per capita library program. Overall, the State provides 
40% of the minimum program and the counties provide 60%. The State/local share of the 
minimum program varies by county depending on local wealth. However, no library board 
may receive less than 20% of the per capita minimum program from the State.  
 
The local share of each library board’s minimum per capita program equals the local 
contribution rate multiplied by local wealth. The local contribution rate is a statewide rate 
representing the counties’ aggregate share of the minimum library program divided by total 
local wealth. Specifically, the local contribution rate equals the overall local share (60%) 
multiplied by the per capita program level multiplied by total State population and divided 
by total State wealth. The State’s share of each library board’s minimum per capita 
program equals the local share subtracted from the product of the per capita program and 
county population. 
 
Required Services 
 
Beginning January 1, 2024, each public library must offer at least one of the following 
services alone or in partnership with units of government or nonprofit organizations:   
 
 
a program to increase scores on the State kindergarten assessment; 
 
a program designed to close learning gaps created by lack of access to education, 
including learning gaps that occurred during the COVID-19 pandemic; 
 
an initiative to expand digital equity and access to free computing and broadband 
Internet; 
 
an educational program providing technology instruction and skills, health literacy, 
instruction on operating a small business, and instruction on workforce 
development; 
 
serving as a community resource and distribution site during public emergencies;

State Expenditures:  Exhibit 1 shows the per capita aid increases under the bill. 
 
The fiscal 2027 budget as introduced includes approximately $52.0 million in general funds 
for public library aid and $10.0 million for regional resource centers. 
 
State formula aid to regional resource centers increases by $302,833 in fiscal 2028 and, 
based upon current projections for population growth, by $1.3 million by fiscal 2031. 
State library program aid increases by $1.6 million in fiscal 2028, and by $6.7 million in 
fiscal 2031. Thus, total direct aid increases by $1.9 million in fiscal 2028, and by 
$8.0 million in fiscal 2031. Fiscal year 2032 is the final year of the statutory phase-in of 
the per capita aid increases under the bill; expenditures thereafter reflect the fully phased-in 
funding level, subject to population changes. 
 
To the extent the additional funding is used to hire personnel and otherwise increase the 
statewide salary base for local library systems, State retirement expenditures may increase 
beginning in fiscal 2030. However, any such impact is not expected to be material. 
 
Local Revenues:  State formula aid to regional resource centers increases by $302,833 in 
fiscal 2028, including an increase of $110,258 for the Eastern Resource Center, $116,645 
for the Southern Resource Center, and $75,931 for the Western Resource Center. State aid 
to regional resource centers increases by $1.3 million by fiscal 2031, including an increase 
of $469,650 for the Eastern Resource Center, $500,384 for the Southern Resource Center, 
and $322,394 for the Western Resource Center.  
 
Local government funding under the library aid formula increases by $1.6 million in 
fiscal 2028 and by $6.7 million in fiscal 2031 as shown in Exhibit 2. Under the bill, 
fiscal year 2032 is the final year of the statutory phase-in of the per capita aid increases. 
 
The increase in the library program per capita amount also increases the required minimum 
local funding amount under the library program. However, under current law, local 
governments tend to fund their local libraries above the minimum required level.

Local Expenditures:  As noted above, the bill requires local libraries to offer certain 
services in lieu of those required under current law. However, the Maryland State Library 
Agency advises the types of services specified in the bill are already performed by local 
libraries throughout the State. The repeal of the existing service requirements is also not 
assumed to affect overall library expenditures. Therefore, this provision is not expected to 
have a material impact on local library expenditures.  
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.
