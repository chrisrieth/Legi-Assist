Ways and Means 
 
 
Public High Schools - Student Transcripts - Traumatic Loss Notation 
 
 
This bill authorizes a high school student who experiences the death of a parent due to 
specified reproductive cancers (e.g., breast cancer, cervical cancer, and uterine cancer) to 
request a standardized traumatic loss notation on the student’s official transcript to reflect 
an impact on academic performance, achievement, or attendance. Local school systems 
must include the notation upon receipt of required documentation. The Maryland State 
Department of Education (MSDE) must develop standard language for the notation that 
maintains student privacy. High school counselors must notify eligible students of the 
option and the State Board of Education (SBE) may adopt regulations. The bill takes effect 
July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  MSDE can develop standard language for the notation that maintains student 
privacy using existing resources. No effect on revenues. 
  
Local Effect:  Local school system expenditures may increase minimally to modify 
transcript systems and process requests for traumatic loss notations authorized under the 
bill; however, any costs are expected to be absorbable within existing resources. No effect 
on revenues. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  A student is eligible to request the notation only if the student submits a 
written request on a form provided by the local school system and documentation verifying

that the parent’s cause of death qualifies under the bill, including a copy of the death 
certificate or a letter from a licensed health care provider. The notation must use the 
standard language developed by MSDE and must identify the school year or years during 
which the student was affected by the traumatic loss. A local school system must remove 
the notation upon the student’s written request. With approval of a surviving parent if the 
student is under age 18, the notation may be accompanied by a letter written by the student 
describing the circumstances of the traumatic loss and its impact. Upon notification that a 
student has experienced a qualifying traumatic loss, a high school counselor must inform 
the student and any surviving parent of the option to request the notation and advise the 
student regarding its potential use in explaining academic fluctuations. 
 
Current Law:  There is no statutory provision that authorizes or requires public high 
schools to add specific notations to a student's official transcript to explain academic 
fluctuations caused by the death of a parent or guardian. Student transcripts, diplomas, and 
graduation requirements are generally governed by local school systems in accordance with 
minimum requirements set by the State Board of Education. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.
