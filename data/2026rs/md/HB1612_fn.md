Ways and Means 
 
 
Sports Wagering - Independent Evaluation of Sports Wagering 
 
 
This bill requires, instead of authorizes as specified in current law, a sports wagering 
licensee or sports wagering operator that advertises in the State to contract with a licensed 
independent evaluator to evaluate and rate the sports wagering licensee’s sports wagering 
content, sports wagering experts, sports wagering influencers, and content partners. 
The bill takes effect July 1, 2026, contingent on the State Lottery and Gaming Control 
Commission (SLGCC) issuing licenses to at least three independent evaluators. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  The bill does not materially affect State finances. If three independent 
evaluators are licensed under the bill, license revenues may potentially increase minimally 
beginning in FY 2027. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Potential meaningful. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Chapter 638 of 2023 requires SLGCC to license independent evaluators to 
evaluate and rate sports wagering content provided by sports wagering experts, sports 
wagering influencers, and content partners. In order to be licensed, an independent 
evaluator must meet specified criteria. A sports wagering licensee or sports wagering 
operator that advertises in the State may contract with a licensed independent evaluator. 
An independent evaluator and its principals and employees may not wager on a sporting 
event. An independent evaluator may not be compensated by a sports wagering licensee or

sports wagering operator that uses the evaluator’s evaluation services solely for marketing 
materials but may be compensated for its evaluation and rating of sports wagering content. 
 
Under COMAR 36.10.06.04, the State Lottery and Gaming Control Agency may issue a 
license for a Tier 1 contractor that is an independent evaluator. For a Tier 1 sports wagering 
contractor, the application fee is $1,500, the license fee is $2,500, and the background 
investigation deposit is $2,000. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Small businesses that are sports wagering licensees must contract 
with a licensed independent evaluator if they wish to continue advertising in the State if 
the bill’s contingency is met. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has been introduced within the last 
three years. See SB 708 and HB 922 of 2025 and SB 1066 and HB 1291 of 2024.
