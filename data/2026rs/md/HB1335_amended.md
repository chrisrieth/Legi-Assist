A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Department of Information Technology – Information Technology Staffing and
3 Capability Assessment –
4 Independent Study

5 For the purpose of requiring the Department of Information Technology to procure the
6 services of a third–party entity to conduct a certain independent study; and generally
7 relating to the staffing, capability assessment, and workforce alignment of information
8 technology and cybersecurity professionals in the State.

9 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
10 That:

11 (a) The Department of Information Technology shall procure the services of a
12 third–party entity to conduct an independent study that includes:

13 (1) an assessment of information technology and cybersecurity staffing
14 needs across the principal departments of the Executive Branch as identified in § 8-201
15 of the State Government Article, including:

16 (i) an inventory of existing information technology and
17 cybersecurity positions by classification, role, and function;

18 (ii) an evaluation of each principal department’s information
19 technology environment, including:

20 1. the size and information technology needs of the
21 principal department;

22 2. the size and scope of systems and applications used by
23 information technology;

24 3. cybersecurity risk profile and regulatory or compliance
25 obligations; and

26 4. legacy system dependencies and modernization
27 requirements;

28 (iii) an analysis of capability gaps between current staffing levels
29 and the skills required to support principal department missions;

30 (iv) identification of the types of information technology and
31 cybersecurity roles necessary to support operations based on the principal department’s
32 specific technology environment; and

33 (v) recommendations for aligning State information technology
34 staffing models with the operational, security, and project delivery needs of principal
35 departments and the strategic priorities of the Department of Information Technology’s
36 statewide information technology master plan; and

37 (2) recommendations for updated information technology classifications
38 and pay scales for information technology and cybersecurity professionals, informed by
39 comparable compensation data from other agencies across State and local government.
