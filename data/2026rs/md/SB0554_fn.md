Baltimore City Board of School Commissioners - Appointment Process - 
Alterations 
 
 
This bill repeals provisions of law establishing the Baltimore City Public School Board 
Community Panel. The bill also requires appointed members of the Baltimore City Board 
of School Commissioners to be subject to confirmation by the Senate of Maryland. This 
requirement applies only to appointed members (and members appointed to fill a vacancy) 
after the bill’s effective date. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The Senate can confirm appointments to the Baltimore City Board of 
School Commissioners using existing resources. 
  
Local Effect:  The bill does not materially affect Baltimore City finances or operations. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Following the 2022 general election, the Baltimore City Board of School 
Commissioners has been restructured as a hybrid appointed and elected board with 
two members elected from the city at large, nine members appointed by the mayor, and 
one student member. Elected members serve a four-year term, appointed members serve a 
three-year term. Members are appointed by the mayor from a list of names provided by the 
Baltimore City Public School Board Community Panel.

The purpose of the Baltimore City Public School Board Community Panel (which the 
mayor must convene) is to select nominees to be recommended to the mayor as qualified 
candidates for appointment to the board (including candidates for vacancies). If the mayor 
elects not to appoint a member or to fill a vacancy from a list submitted by the panel, the 
mayor must reconvene the panel to submit additional names of qualified candidates. Under 
the bill, the panel is repealed, thereby eliminating its role in the appointment process. 
 
There are 20 elected school boards and four hybrid boards in the State.  
The Appendix – Local Boards of Education Membership shows the selection methods, 
terms of office, and membership of the 24 local boards of education. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Recent Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.

Appendix – Local Boards of Education Membership 
 
 
The composition of the local boards of education varies with members serving three- to 
four-year terms. Twenty counties have elected school boards and four counties have 
combined appointed and elected school boards. All 24 boards have either student members 
or student representatives who regularly present at board meetings; however, only 8 boards 
allow student members to vote, which generally excludes authority to vote on matters 
relating to collective bargaining and personnel. Three counties with voting student 
members also preclude student members from voting on matters relating to operating and 
capital budgets. Exhibit 1 shows the selection methods, terms of office, and membership 
of the 24 local school boards.
