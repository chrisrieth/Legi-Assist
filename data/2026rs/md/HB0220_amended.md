CHAPTER ______

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Environment – Water – Individual Submeters

3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the installation of individual water submeters
4 for certain apartment houses and mobile home parks; prohibiting the
5 owner, operator, or manager of an apartment house or a mobile home park, or a
6 contractor hired by the owner, operator, or manager, from imposing certain costs on
7 a unit; authorizing the owner, operator, or manager of an apartment house or a mobile
8 home park to bill an occupant a certain amount under certain circumstances;
9 prohibiting the owner, operator, or manager of an apartment house or a mobile home
10 park from billing an occupant under certain circumstances; requiring the owner,
11 operator, or manager of an apartment house or a mobile home park, or a contractor
12 hired by the owner, operator, or manager to maintain certain records; establishing a
13 certain complaint process; requiring certain landlords to use a certain written lease,
14 provide a copy of a water or sewer bill to a tenant within a certain time frame, and
15 notify prospective tenants of certain information within a certain time frame;
16 prohibiting certain landlords from requiring a tenant to make certain payments to a
17 third party; and generally relating to individual water submeters.

28 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
29 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

30 Article – Environment

31 9–1115.

32 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
33 INDICATED.

34 (2) “ADEQUATE RECORDS” INCLUDES:

35 (I) A COPY OF ALL CHARGES THAT THE WATER SERVICE
36 PROVIDER IMPOSED ON THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT
37 HOUSE OR A MOBILE HOME PARK IN THE PRECEDING 2 YEARS;

38 (II) THE TOTAL UTILITY COST IMPOSED ON ALL UNITS IN THE
39 FACILITY EACH MONTH FOR THE CURRENT CALENDAR YEAR AND PREVIOUS
40 CALENDAR YEAR;

41 (III) THE TOTAL REVENUE COLLECTED FROM OCCUPANTS TO
42 PAY THE CHARGES IMPOSED ON THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER BY THE
43 WATER SERVICE PROVIDER EACH MONTH FOR THE CURRENT CALENDAR YEAR AND
44 PREVIOUS CALENDAR YEAR;

45 (IV) AN EXPLANATION OF THE FORMULA USED TO ALLOCATE
46 THE COST OF EACH UNIT’S WATER CONSUMPTION; AND

47 (V) ANY OTHER INFORMATION NECESSARY FOR A TENANT TO
48 VERIFY A WATER UTILITY BILL.

49 (3) (I) “APARTMENT HOUSE OR A MOBILE HOME PARK” MEANS ONE OR
50 MORE BUILDINGS THAT EACH CONTAIN MORE THAN TWO DWELLING UNITS AND IN
51 WHICH ALL THE DWELLING UNITS ARE OCCUPIED PRIMARILY FOR
52 NONTRANSIENT USE WITH RENT PAID AT INTERVALS OF 1 WEEK OR LONGER.

53 (II) “APARTMENT HOUSE” INCLUDES A RESIDENTIAL
54 CONDOMINIUM OR COOPERATIVE, WHETHER THE UNITS ARE RENTED OR OWNER
55 OCCUPIED.

56 (4) “DWELLING UNIT” MEANS PREMISES THAT CONSIST OF ONE OR
57 MORE ROOMS SUITABLE FOR OCCUPANCY AS A RESIDENCE AND THAT CONTAIN
58 KITCHEN AND BATHROOM FACILITIES.

59 (5) “INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER” MEANS EQUIPMENT USED
60 TO DETERMINE THE ACTUAL USE OF WATER FOR EACH RESIDENTIAL UNIT IN AN
61 APARTMENT HOUSE OR A MOBILE HOME PARK.

62 (6) “WATER SERVICE PROVIDER” MEANS, AS APPLICABLE:

63 (I) A POLITICAL SUBDIVISION THAT PROVIDES WATER AND
64 SEWERAGE SERVICES UNDER SUBTITLE 7 OF THIS TITLE;

65 (II) A WATER COMPANY, AS DEFINED IN § 1–101 OF THE PUBLIC
66 UTILITIES ARTICLE;

67 (III) THE WASHINGTON SUBURBAN SANITARY COMMISSION; OR

68 (IV) A MOBILE HOME PARK.

69 (B) SUBJECT TO THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION, AND WITH THE
70 APPROVAL OF THE DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT
71 AND THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS OR EQUIVALENT LOCAL AUTHORITY, A LOCAL
72 HOUSING AUTHORITY ESTABLISHED UNDER DIVISION II OF THE HOUSING AND
73 COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT ARTICLE MAY INSTALL INDIVIDUAL SUBMETERS
74 FOR ANY COMBINATION OF APARTMENT HOUSES OR DWELLING UNITS.

75 (C) AN OWNER, AN OPERATOR, OR A MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT HOUSE
76 OR A MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
77 MANAGER, MAY INSTALL AN INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER FOR EACH
78 DWELLING UNIT THAT IS NOT DIRECTLY METERED FOR WATER TO
79 ALLOCATE FAIRLY THE COST OF EACH UNIT’S WATER CONSUMPTION.

80 (D) (1) AN OWNER, AN OPERATOR, OR A MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT
81 HOUSE OR A MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER,
82 OPERATOR, OR MANAGER, WHO INSTALLS INDIVIDUAL SUBMETERS
83 UNDER THIS SECTION TO PROVIDE BULK METERED SERVICE MAY NOT IMPOSE ON A
84 UNIT IN THE FACILITY ANY WATER OR SEWERAGE COST EXCEPT THE CHARGES THAT
85 THE WATER SERVICE PROVIDER ACTUALLY IMPOSES ON THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
86 MANAGER.

87 (2) THE CHARGES IMPOSED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
88 SUBSECTION SHALL BE ALLOCATED AMONG THE UNITS IN PROPORTION TO THE
89 ACTUAL USAGE BY THE UNIT.

90 (3) THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT HOUSE
91 OR A MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
92 MANAGER, MAY NOT IMPOSE ON A UNIT IN THE FACILITY ANY COSTS ASSOCIATED
93 WITH:

94 (I) POOR MAINTENANCE OR LEAKS THAT THE OWNER,
95 OPERATOR, MANAGER, OR CONTRACTOR IS RESPONSIBLE FOR; OR

96 (II) COMMON AREA USAGE; OR

97 (III) USAGE IN ANY PART OF A BUILDING THAT IS NOT USED AS A
98 RESIDENTIAL UNIT.

99 (4) THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT HOUSE
100 OR A MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
101 MANAGER, MAY COLLECT AN ADDITIONAL SERVICE CHARGE NOT EXCEEDING $1 PER
102 UNIT PER MONTH TO COVER ADMINISTRATIVE COSTS AND BILLING.

103 (E) (1) EACH INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER UNDER THIS SECTION
104 IS SUBJECT TO THE REGULATIONS AND STANDARDS THAT THE DEPARTMENT, IN
105 CONSULTATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND COMMUNITY
106 DEVELOPMENT, ADOPTS FOR THE ACCURACY, TESTING, AND RECORD KEEPING OF
107 INDIVIDUAL SUBMETERS.

108 (2) REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION SHALL BE AT
109 LEAST AS STRINGENT AS THE REGULATIONS APPLICABLE TO THE WATER METERS
110 THAT WATER SERVICE PROVIDERS INSTALL.

111 (F) (1) (I) IN THIS SUBSECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
112 MEANINGS INDICATED.

113 (II) “ACTUAL USAGE” MEANS USAGE THAT WAS MEASURED
114 WHEN A METER WAS CONSIDERED ACCURATE.

115 (III) “ESTIMATED USAGE” MEANS THE AVERAGE USAGE BY
116 DWELLING UNITS IN THE SAME APARTMENT HOUSE THAT:

117 1. ARE OF COMPARABLE SIZE; AND

118 2. HAVE A COMPARABLE NUMBER OF OCCUPANTS.

119 (2) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION, IF AN
120 INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER DOES NOT PROVIDE ADEQUATE OR ACCURATE DATA FOR
121 BILLING PURPOSES, THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT
122 HOUSE MAY BILL THE OCCUPANT OF THE UNIT:

123 (I) THE AVERAGE COST OF THE MOST RECENT 3 MONTHS OF
124 ACTUAL USAGE BY THE OCCUPANT OF THE UNIT; OR

125 (II) IF RECORDS OF THE MOST RECENT 3 MONTHS OF ACTUAL
126 USAGE BY THE OCCUPANT OF THE UNIT ARE NOT AVAILABLE, THE AVERAGE COST
127 OF THE MOST RECENT 3 MONTHS OF ESTIMATED USAGE.

128 (3) IF AN INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER HAS NOT BEEN REPAIRED OR
129 REPLACED FOR TWO CONSECUTIVE BILLING CYCLES, THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
130 MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT HOUSE OR A MOBILE HOME PARK MAY NOT BILL THE
131 OCCUPANT OF THAT UNIT UNTIL THE INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER IS REPAIRED OR
132 REPLACED.

133 (G) THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF THE APARTMENT HOUSE OR
134 MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
135 MANAGER, SHALL:

136 (1) MAINTAIN ADEQUATE RECORDS REGARDING INDIVIDUAL
137 SUBMETERS;

138 (2) OBTAIN ANY ADDITIONAL RECORDS NEEDED BY AN OCCUPANT OF
139 A UNIT TO VERIFY A WATER BILL; AND

140 (3) ALLOW THE OCCUPANT OF THE UNIT TO INSPECT THE RECORDS
141 DURING REASONABLE BUSINESS HOURS.

142 (H) (1) A COMPLAINT BY AN OCCUPANT OF A DWELLING UNIT
143 AGAINST AN OWNER, OPERATOR, OR MANAGER OF AN APARTMENT HOUSE OR A
144 MOBILE HOME PARK, OR A CONTRACTOR HIRED BY THE OWNER, OPERATOR, OR
145 MANAGER, UNDER THIS SECTION MAY BE FILED IN THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY
146 WHERE THE APARTMENT HOUSE OR MOBILE HOME PARK IS LOCATED.

147 (2) A COMPLAINT FILED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
148 SUBSECTION MAY BE HANDLED BY:

149 (I) THE LANDLORD–TENANT COMMISSION, IF ONE EXISTS, OF
150 THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY;

151 (II) THE CONSUMER PROTECTION AGENCY, IF ONE EXISTS, OF
152 THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION IF THERE IS NOT A LANDLORD–TENANT
153 COMMISSION IN THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY OR IF THE COMMISSION DOES NOT
154 HAVE JURISDICTION;

155 (III) THE CONSUMER PROTECTION DIVISION OF THE OFFICE OF
156 THE ATTORNEY GENERAL, IF THERE IS NOT A CONSUMER PROTECTION AGENCY IN
157 THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY OR THE AGENCY DOES NOT HAVE JURISDICTION; OR

158 (IV) A PRIVATE CAUSE OF ACTION.

159 (3) NOTHING IN THIS SUBSECTION SHALL BE CONSTRUED AS
160 PREVENTING ANY STATE AGENCY FROM EXERCISING ITS AUTHORITY REGARDING
161 THE COLLECTION OF OBLIGATIONS ARISING FROM CONSUMER TRANSACTIONS.

162 Article – Real Property

163 8–205.3.

164 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER” HAS THE MEANING STATED
165 IN § 9–1115 OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE.

166 (B) A LANDLORD THAT REQUIRES A TENANT TO MAKE PAYMENTS FOR
167 WATER OR SEWER SERVICES MEASURED BY AN INDIVIDUAL SUBMETER TO A THIRD
168 PARTY SHALL:

169 (1) USE A WRITTEN LEASE THAT PROVIDES NOTICE THAT THE
170 TENANT IS RESPONSIBLE FOR MAKING PAYMENTS FOR THE WATER OR SEWER
171 SERVICES TO THE THIRD PARTY;

172 (2) PROVIDE A COPY OF THE WATER OR SEWER BILL TO THE TENANT
173 NOT LATER THAN 20 DAYS BEFORE PAYMENT OF THE BILL IS DUE; AND

174 (3) BEFORE THE BEGINNING OF THE INITIAL LEASE, NOTIFY THE
175 PROSPECTIVE TENANT IN WRITING OF:

176 (I) THE TOTAL WATER AND SEWER COSTS BILLED TO THE
177 OCCUPANTS OF THE UNIT IN THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING 2 YEARS; OR

178 (II) IF WATER AND SEWER COSTS WERE NOT BILLED TO AN
179 OCCUPANT OF THE UNIT FOR THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING 2 YEARS, THE TOTAL
180 WATER AND SEWER COSTS BILLED TO A COMPARABLY SIZED UNIT WITHIN THAT TIME
181 PERIOD.

182 (C) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A LANDLORD THAT
183 DOES NOT MEET THE REQUIREMENTS OF § 9–1115 OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE
184 MAY NOT REQUIRE A TENANT TO MAKE PAYMENTS TO A THIRD PARTY FOR WATER
185 OR SEWER SERVICES.

186 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
187 October 1, 2026.
